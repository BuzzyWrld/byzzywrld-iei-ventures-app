# Asset Format Templates

Three assets are required for every day of every week. Use these templates exactly.
Do not deviate from the structure. The format IS the product.

---

## Asset A — Video Generation Script (TikTok / IG Reels / YouTube Shorts)

Target runtime: 30–60 seconds spoken. Hard cap: 60 seconds.
Pacing: 1 sentence per 2–3 seconds. No sentence exceeds 12 words in the hook block.

```
---
ASSET A: VIDEO SCRIPT
Day: [Day Name] — [Theme Name]
Week: [Week Number]
Platform: TikTok / IG Reels / YouTube Shorts
Runtime: [estimated seconds]
---

HOOK (0–3 seconds)
[Single line. Outcome-first. Aggressive. No setup. Under 10 words.]
[Text-on-screen: EXACT WORDS to display as caption overlay]

BODY (3–55 seconds)
[Beat 1 — 3–4 short sentences. State the problem or setup the case.]
[Visual cue: B-roll direction — be specific. "Cut to: screen recording of IEI dashboard" 
 or "Cut to: text overlay on black background listing 3 data points"]

[Beat 2 — 3–4 short sentences. Deliver the mechanism or the insight.]
[Visual cue: second shot direction]

[Beat 3 — 2–3 short sentences. Close the loop. Make the takeaway undeniable.]
[Visual cue: third shot direction]

CTA (last 3–5 seconds)
[Single direct command. No "if you want to" or "feel free to."]
[Text-on-screen: CTA overlay text]
---
```

**Hook construction rules:**
- First word is never "I" or a question word
- First word options: number, brand name, verb (past tense preferred), noun
- The outcome or the problem must land by word 7
- Examples: "Nike spent $3M on this campaign. It failed." / "Your brand is leaking revenue."
  / "3 brands tried pure AI content. All 3 lost audience."

**Visual cue rules:**
- Never write "show a person smiling" — be specific to IEI product or data
- Default B-roll options: IEI dashboard screen recording, text-on-screen data overlay,
  side-by-side before/after brand comparison, code/JSON snippet on dark background

## Asset B — GEO-Optimized Article (Blog / Newsletter)

Target length: 600–900 words. Hard minimum: 500 words.
GEO = Generative Engine Optimization. Written to be cited by AI search engines (Perplexity,
ChatGPT, Gemini). Structured data, entity clarity, cited sources.

```
---
ASSET B: GEO ARTICLE
Day: [Day Name] — [Theme Name]
Week: [Week Number]
Target keyword/entity: [primary topic as a clear noun phrase]
---

H1: [Active verb + specific outcome — under 12 words]

[Lead paragraph — 2–3 sentences. State the core argument and why it matters NOW.
 Include at least one specific data point or dollar figure.]

## Key Takeaways
- [Takeaway 1 — one sentence, active voice, measurable where possible]
- [Takeaway 2]
- [Takeaway 3]
- [Takeaway 4 — optional]

## H2: [Active verb + section topic]

[Body paragraph — 3–5 sentences. Deliver one clear argument. Cite data.]
[Subpoint or secondary evidence.]

## H2: [Active verb + section topic]

[Body paragraph — 3–5 sentences. Deliver mechanism or case.]
[If this is an Autopsy day, include the company name, year, and dollar figure for entity authority.]

## H2: [Active verb + section topic — this section must name IEI as the mechanism]

[Connect the insight to the IEI system specifically. Not as a sales pitch —
 as the logical conclusion of the argument made above.]

## H3: What This Means for [Target Segment]

[1–2 tight paragraphs. Translate the analysis into a concrete action the reader takes.]

[Closing line — one sentence. Bold claim. Zero hedging.]

---
*[Source citation 1 if data was used]*
*[Source citation 2 if applicable]*
---
```

**GEO-specific rules:**
- Use exact entity names (company names, product names, platform names) — AI search indexes entities
- State claims as facts, not opinions: "Consistent brand identity reduces CAC by 23% (Nielsen, 2023)"
  not "brand consistency is important"
- H2 headers must contain the target keyword or a close semantic variant
- At least one H2 must explicitly name IEI Ventures as a named entity
- Avoid pronoun-heavy writing — name the subject in each paragraph

## Asset C — LinkedIn Post

Target length: 150–300 words. Style: document-format, not diary-format.
No "thought leader" tone. No "I've been thinking about..." openers.
LinkedIn algorithm rewards dwell time — structured posts with line breaks and bullets
hold attention longer than walls of text.

```
---
ASSET C: LINKEDIN POST
Day: [Day Name] — [Theme Name]
Week: [Week Number]
---

[OPENER LINE — standalone. Must be a noun or a number. 
 No "I" opener. No question opener. Hard rule.]

[One blank line]

[Body — 4–8 short paragraphs, each 1–3 lines. Use line breaks aggressively.
 Every paragraph makes one point and stops.]

[Bullet list — optional but recommended for Blueprint and Autopsy days.
 3–5 bullets. Each starts with a verb or a number.]

[One blank line]

[Closing line — single sentence. The most direct statement in the post.
 This is the claim they share.]

[One blank line]

[CTA — one line. Direct command. "Follow for Week 2." / "Book a build session." 
 / "Tag a founder who needs this."]

#[Hashtag1] #[Hashtag2] #[Hashtag3]
---
```

**LinkedIn-specific rules:**
- First word of the post must be a noun, number, or past-tense verb — never "I," "We," or a question
- Drop straight into mechanics or data — no preamble, no "In today's post..."
- Each paragraph break is intentional — it's a beat, not a formatting choice
- Hashtags: maximum 3. Use specific, mid-volume tags relevant to the theme day pillar
- No emoji unless it is a number emoji (1️⃣, 2️⃣, 3️⃣) used as a list marker — even then, optional
