# 7-Day Content Matrix

Each day has a fixed theme, a mapped pillar, and a set of variety rules that must be enforced
across weeks to prevent hook repetition and audience fatigue.

---

## Daily Theme Definitions

### Monday — THE SIGNAL (Current Events & Marketing Analysis)
- **Focus:** Data-backed analysis of the weekend's biggest marketing news or campaign launches.
  Explain why it worked or failed based on brand infrastructure — not opinions, infrastructure gaps.
- **Primary Pillar:** Pillar 2 (ROI Equation) with occasional Pillar 1 (Blueprint)
- **Tone anchor:** Analyst voice. State what happened, what the data shows, what it means for
  the ICA. No speculation. Numbers first.
- **Hook pattern:** "[Brand/Campaign] [did X]. The infrastructure reason: [specific gap]."
- **Variety rule:** Each Monday hook must name a different industry than the prior Monday.

### Tuesday — THE BLUEPRINT (Sequential Workflows)
- **Focus:** A 3-part tactical micro-series. A single automated workflow broken into
  Part 1 / Part 2 / Part 3, each a standalone asset but part of a sequence.
- **Primary Pillar:** Pillar 1 (Blueprint)
- **Tone anchor:** Operator voice. Teaching someone how a system works, step by step.
  Every step is a numbered action, not a concept.
- **Hook pattern:** "[Step X of 3]: [Single outcome this step achieves]."
- **Variety rule:** Each Tuesday must cover a different workflow category
  (brand → content → distribution → analytics → automation → sales → onboarding).

### Wednesday — THE AI PARADOX (Controversies & Hybrid Systems)
- **Focus:** Polarizing takes on why pure AI marketing fails and why the human-in-the-loop
  engine wins. Pick a fight with an assumption the ICA holds.
- **Primary Pillar:** Pillar 4 (AI Paradox)
- **Tone anchor:** Challenger voice. State the unpopular position in the first sentence.
  Back it with data or a specific failure case.
- **Hook pattern:** "[Popular belief]. [Why it's wrong]. [What actually works]."
- **Variety rule:** Each Wednesday must attack a different AI assumption
  (AI for copywriting → AI for brand → AI for ads → AI for SEO → AI for strategy).

### Thursday — THE AUTOPSY (Historical Case Studies & Throwbacks)
- **Focus:** Deep-dive tear-down of a historical brand win or failure. The connection to brand
  infrastructure must be explicit — what was missing or what was built right.
- **Primary Pillar:** Pillar 3 (Autopsy)
- **Tone anchor:** Forensic voice. Diagnose, not judge. "Here is what the data shows was missing."
- **Hook pattern:** "[Company] [won/lost] $[X] because of one brand infrastructure decision."
- **Variety rule:** Alternate between wins and failures week over week. No same company twice
  within a 4-week block.

### Friday — THE SANDBOX (Product Proof)
- **Focus:** "Watch us break our own tool." Live product demonstration handling an absurd or
  highly complex prompt. Proves speed, range, and quality without a sales pitch.
- **Primary Pillar:** Pillar 1 (Blueprint) + Pillar 2 (ROI)
- **Tone anchor:** Builder voice. Confident, slightly irreverent. "We tried to break it. We couldn't."
- **Hook pattern:** "We gave the IEI Dashboard [absurd/complex prompt]. Here's what it shipped in [time]."
- **Variety rule:** Each Friday must use a different product capability
  (brand kit → content calendar → landing page → logo system → pitch deck → email kit → dev brief).

### Saturday — THE HITLIST (B2B Call-Outs & Agency Partnerships)
- **Focus:** Direct, named call-outs to B2B platforms (Vendasta, HubSpot agencies, SaaS integrators)
  identifying their exact bottleneck and proposing the IEI white-label solution.
- **Primary Pillar:** Pillar 6 (Channel Takeover)
- **Tone anchor:** Partnership voice. Not a fight — a diagnosis with a solution attached.
  "You have this problem. We built the fix."
- **Hook pattern:** "@[Platform/Agency type]: you're losing [revenue/clients/time] because [specific gap]."
- **Variety rule:** Each Saturday targets a different platform category or agency type.
  Avoid tagging the same platform twice in a 4-week block.

### Sunday — THE GROWTH ENGINE (UGC, Affiliates, Testimonials)
- **Focus:** Targeting the Sunday afternoon hustle/planning crowd. Affiliate success stories,
  testimonials, and side-revenue opportunities. Community-building content that converts passive
  followers into active affiliates.
- **Primary Pillar:** Pillar 5 (UGC & Affiliate Engine)
- **Tone anchor:** Proof voice. Let results speak. Real numbers from real people.
  If a number is unavailable, frame the mechanism and invite the audience to share theirs.
- **Hook pattern:** "[Name/role] was doing [X]. Now doing [Y]. Here's the exact system they used."
- **Variety rule:** Alternate between affiliate success stories, testimonials, and side-revenue
  opportunity framing week over week.

## Cross-Week Variety Rules

Enforced by the self-audit in Step 4 (Week 4 assembly):

1. **No hook opens with the same first word twice within a week** — if Monday opens with "Your,"
   no other day that week can open with "Your"
2. **No hook uses the same structural pattern on consecutive weeks for the same day** —
   if Monday Week 1 is a question, Monday Week 2 must be a statement
3. **Industry coverage must rotate** — across 4 weeks, the 4 Monday Signals must cover
   4 different industries
4. **Company names in Autopsies must not repeat** within a 4-week block
5. **Friday product capability must not repeat** within a 4-week block
6. **At least 2 of the 4 weeks must include a brand that is not US-based** in Thursday Autopsies
