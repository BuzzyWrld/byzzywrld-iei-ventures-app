01

The Brand Story

What Pen2Purpose stands for and why the visual identity was built the way it was.

Mission

To gather writers who believe that words are tools of purpose — not just expression. A space to sharpen craft, share voice, and hold each other accountable to the page.

Visual Philosophy

The etching-style illustration references literary salons and the handwritten tradition. Dark indigo grounds the brand in depth and seriousness. Gold signals value and intentionality — never trend.

The logo is a sealed mark. It carries the entire brand identity — treat it accordingly.

On parchment and ivory backgrounds. Print collateral.

Monochrome

05

Logo Rules

The mark took time to earn. Protect it accordingly.

Do

  • Use on dark indigo or parchment backgrounds exclusively
  • Maintain the circular frame — never crop or cut it
  • Keep the gold arc text legible at all sizes
  • Use monochrome version on embroidered or stamped materials
  • Pair with IM Fell English wordmark in headlines

Don't

  • Place on busy photography without a dark overlay
  • Stretch, skew, or rotate the mark
  • Recolor the illustration or change gold to another accent
  • Remove the circular border and use illustration alone
  • Pair with sans-serif fonts in the same composition

08

Design Elements

Recurring graphic motifs that extend the identity beyond the logo.

Gold Rule

Horizontal rule lines used as section breaks, decorative dividers, and framing devices.

Circle Motif

The circular logo shape echoes as ghost rings in backgrounds and as bullet/list markers.

Etching Reference

Illustrations used alongside the brand should feel hand-drawn or etched — not digital/flat. Line weights vary. No solid fills.

Grain Overlay

Applied at 4–8% opacity on dark backgrounds. Adds depth and prevents flat digital feel. Never on light backgrounds.
