Brand Archetype & Voice

The personality that bridges scrappy founders and institutional wealth

PRIMARY ARCHETYPE

The Sage-Magician Hybrid

Sage: Trusted advisor, expert guide, wisdom-driven — "We've seen what works and what doesn't."

Magician: Transformational, visionary, possibility-expanding — "We make the impossible fundable."

Real-world equivalent: A senior partner at a top-tier firm who also founded two successful startups. Credible enough for institutional LPs, approachable enough for first-time founders.

BRAND PERSONALITY

Authoritative

Not arrogant

Transparent

Not naive

Visionary

Not speculative

Supportive

Not coddling

VOICE GUIDELINES

Tone: Authoritative

Speak with conviction. Use declarative sentences. Avoid hedging language ("maybe," "sort of," "we think").

✓ "The Scorecard changes how diligence works."

✗ "We believe scorecards might help with diligence."

Tone: Transparent

Show the data. Name the problem. Don't hide behind corporate-speak or vague promises.

✓ "58% of investors fund transparent founders faster."

✗ "Transparency is important to investors."

Tone: Direct

Cut the fluff. Get to the point. Respect the reader's time — especially founders and investors.

✓ "Raise capital without gatekeepers."

✗ "Our innovative platform helps facilitate capital access."

Product Positioning

Four core offerings, each solving a specific friction point

PRODUCT 01

The Scorecard Protocol

Standardized startup profiles with real-time milestone tracking. Technology readiness, climate impact, team experience, business model, funding roadmap — all in one scannable format.

Pain Solved: Investors can't compare early-stage startups because everyone's deck is different.

PRODUCT 02

Mentor Network

Curated senior executives from climate tech and investment industries. On-demand office hours, cohort-style courses, strategic validation.

Pain Solved: First-time founders don't know what they don't know. Mentorship triples success rates.

PRODUCT 03

SPV & Syndication Tools

Built-in SPV creation for investors who want to pool capital. Instant syndicate formation without the legal overhead of traditional vehicles.

Pain Solved: Angels and small funds can't easily co-invest without expensive SPV setup.

PRODUCT 04

Wone Academy

Courses taught by industry mentors. Completion badges visible on scorecards. Investors see which founders have invested in their own education.

Pain Solved: No credentialing system for founder readiness outside of expensive accelerators.

Section 13

Visual/Aesthetic Direction

Evolving the current visual language to match a late-stage, high-valuation tech company

CURRENT STATE (TO RETIRE)
  • Generic tech blue gradients
  • Placeholder images and stock photos
  • Inconsistent typography hierarchy
  • Testimonials without real names/photos
  • Tagline drift (3 different taglines in circulation)
  • Founder bios still placeholder text on site
ELEVATED STATE (TARGET)
  • Deep midnight + clarity teal palette — institutional + innovative
  • Real advisor photos, real portfolio company logos
  • Strict Space Grotesk + DM Sans + IBM Plex Mono stack
  • Data-forward design — stats, metrics, proof points visible
  • Unified tagline: "Capital Finds Clarity."
  • Complete founder bios leveraging institutional credibility

VISUAL DESIGN PRINCIPLES

◐

Contrast

Dark fields with electric accents. Never muddy.

▤

Data-Forward

Show the numbers. Metrics > adjectives.

◰

Negative Space

Confident brands don't crowd. Let elements breathe.

◈

Card-Based

Information in containers. Never floating text.

VISUAL REFERENCE: INFRASTRUCTURE BRANDS

Study the visual language of Stripe, Plaid, Mercury, and Ramp. These are the benchmarks for "financial infrastructure" aesthetics — dark fields, metric-forward hero sections, minimal illustration, maximum credibility. Wone should feel like it belongs in this category, not in the crowdfunding/accelerator visual space.
