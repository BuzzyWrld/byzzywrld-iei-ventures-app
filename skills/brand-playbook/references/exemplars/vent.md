## 01 — Brand Identity

NAME · ESSENCE · MISSION · VISION · VALUES · STORY · PERSONA
*WS2 — BRAND IDENTITY*

### Core Identity

**BRAND NAME**
VENT

**TAGLINE**
"say it. lose it."

**BRAND ESSENCE — ONE WORD**
RELEASE

**MISSION**
To give everyone a private, consequence-free space to release emotional pressure — without storage, without judgment, without a trace.

**VISION**
A world where unexpressed emotion doesn't accumulate into chronic stress — where the act of release is as accessible as breathing.

### Core Values

**01 Radical Privacy**
No data. No storage. No surveillance. The product is the promise.

**02 Non-Performative**
Vent refuses the attention economy. Nothing here is for anyone else.

**03 Honest Design**
Every feature earns its place by deepening release, not extracting engagement.

### The Vent Story

> "Most apps want your data. Most platforms want your attention. Most wellness products want your gratitude. Vent wants nothing from you — except for you to let it out."

We built Vent because the world has no container for the scream you're not allowed to scream. Not at work. Not at home. Not anywhere that has ears. Every emotion has to go somewhere — and if it doesn't go out, it goes in. Vent exists at that fault line: the moment before swallowing it, the moment before saying something you can't unsay. You open the app. You speak. You shout. You cry. And then it's gone. No trace. No record. No one heard it but the void. That's the design. That's the whole design.

### Brand Personality

**THREE WORDS:** FIERCE · PRIVATE · CATHARTIC

**ARCHETYPE:** The Liberator
Breaks chains, holds no grudges, leaves no record. Not a healer. Not a judge. A release valve.

**VOICE & TONE**

| VENT IS | VENT IS NOT |
|---|---|
| Blunt · Spare · Unapologetic · Truthful · Dark but not nihilistic | Soft · Therapeutic · Wordy · Motivational · Bright |

## 03 — Visual System

COLOR · TYPOGRAPHY · LOGO SYSTEM · USAGE RULES
*WS4 — COLOR PALETTE · THREE-AXIS SELECTION*

### The Palette

Axis 1: Tech/Bold archetype → near-black, electric accent. Axis 2: ICA 22–38, emotionally intelligent, urban → high-contrast, unsentimental. Axis 3: Psychology → black = power/authority, orange-red = urgency/release, bone = breath/rest.

| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| The Void | `#080808` | Primary. Backgrounds, negative space. The absence that makes release possible. |
| Ember | `#FF3C00` | Accent. CTAs, record button, active states. The heat of what's being released. |
| Ash | `#1A1A1A` | Secondary bg. Cards, surfaces, elevated elements. |
| Bone | `#F0EDE8` | Text on dark. Also primary bg for light-field pages. Warmth without softness. |
| Smoke | `#A0A0A0` | Muted text, secondary info, labels. The whisper beneath the shout. |
| Char | `#2E2E2E` | Borders, dividers, inactive states. The line between. |

**DO NOT USE**
Pure white (#FFFFFF) — use Bone instead. Pastels of any kind — violates the brand archetype. Purple or teal — too wellness/tech adjacent. No gradients except the specific gradient divider treatment.

### Typography — Three Typefaces

- **Bebas Neue — Display** · 400 ONLY · Logo, section headers, hero text, price points — never below 18px.
- **DM Sans — Body** · 300 / 400 / 500 weights · Paragraphs, descriptions, UI text — minimum 13px.
- **Space Mono — Accent / Label** · 400 / 700 · Eyebrows, captions, data labels, countdown timers — minimum 10px.

**PAIRING RULES**
Bebas Neue never appears below 18px · DM Sans never appears in all-caps · Space Mono is never used for long-form body copy · Do not pair Bebas Neue and Space Mono at the same hierarchy level · Italic is permitted in Space Mono only for pull quotes and taglines.

### Logo System

Type: Wordmark + Lettermark. Geometric anchor: horizontal baseline with circular dot accent. Passes black-on-white master rule.

Variants:
- PRIMARY — DARK FIELD: `VENT`
- REVERSED — LIGHT FIELD: `VENT`
- MONOCHROME / 1-COLOR: `VENT`
- LETTERMARK — APP ICON: `V`
- ACCENT ONLY: `V·ENT`
- FORBIDDEN: outlined `VENT`

**MINIMUM SIZES**
Digital: 80px wide · Print: 0.75 inches · App icon: 1024×1024px source · Clear space = cap-height of "V" on all sides.

**FORBIDDEN TREATMENTS**
No drop shadows · No gradients on logo · No rotation · No color substitutions · No stretching · No outlines.

## 06 — Target Audience

DEMOGRAPHICS · PSYCHOGRAPHICS · BEHAVIORAL PROFILE · BUYING BEHAVIOR
*WS7 — TARGET AUDIENCE + CUSTOMER PERSONA*

### The Vent User

**PERSONA NAME:** The Contained One

**DEMOGRAPHICS**
Age: 22–42 · Gender: All, skews female 60% · Income: $35K–$120K · Urban/suburban · Smartphone-native · Works in environments with emotional suppression norms (corporate, caregiving, creative industries).

**PSYCHOGRAPHICS**
Emotionally intelligent but emotionally managed. Aware of their suppression. Skeptical of wellness culture's demands. Values privacy deeply. Doesn't trust platforms with their data. Has tried journaling and found it performative.

**BEHAVIORAL PROFILE**
Deletes apps that ask for too much. Reads privacy policies. Has a therapist or has considered one. Scrolls social media to decompress but feels worse after. Talks to close friends about surface-level things. Keeps the real stuff to themselves.

**BUYING TRIGGERS**
- "No data" in the first sentence
- Frictionless install (no account required)
- Word of mouth from trusted source
- Genuine press coverage (not influencer)
- Free tier that actually works

**TRAIT TAGS**
PRIVACY-FIRST · HIGH EQ · WELLNESS-SKEPTIC · URBAN · OVERLOADED · UNDEREXPRESSED

## 08 — Niche Markets

SEGMENTS · TRENDS · CUSTOMER NEEDS · PRICING · DISTRIBUTION
*WS9 — NICHE MARKETS*

### Five Segments

**Corporate Burnout Market**
Office workers and managers who cannot express frustration in professional settings. Rising stress in hybrid work culture. Pricing: Free → Release tier. Distribution: LinkedIn content, HR channel. Key insight: They have the credit card and the suppression habit.

**Mental Health Adjacent**
People in therapy, or on waitlists for therapy, who need a between-session pressure valve. Psychologists may even recommend Vent. Pricing: All tiers. Distribution: Therapist community, Psychology Today, mental health TikTok.

**Enterprise Wellness Buyers**
HR and People Ops leaders at companies 500+ seeking scalable, anonymous emotional tools. Growing EAP budget allocation. Pricing: Per-seat enterprise ($5–$15/user).

## 09 — Dream 100

ICA DESIRES · 12 PAIN POINTS · WHERE THEY'RE HIDING
*WS10 — DREAM 100*

**ICA: The Contained One**
Desires: To feel relief without consequences. To have somewhere private to be fully human. To stop carrying so much alone.

### 12 Core Pain Points

1. Can't express anger at work without career consequences
2. Can't "lose it" in front of kids, parents, or partner
3. No trusted person to hear the full, unfiltered truth
4. Therapy waitlist is 3 months long and the crisis is now
5. Social media makes feelings performative, not cathartic
6. Journaling feels like homework, not release
7. Venting to friends creates obligation and worry
8. Suppression turns into physical tension, headaches, insomnia
9. Privacy anxiety — nothing digital feels truly private
10. Existing wellness apps require compliance, calm, and growth
11. Feeling like emotions are "too much" for anyone to hold
12. No tool exists that asks nothing, takes nothing, gives release
