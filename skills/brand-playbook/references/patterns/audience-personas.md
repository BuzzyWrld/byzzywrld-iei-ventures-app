# Audience & Persona Patterns

> Distilled from 9 IEI training documents. Use this when generating Ideal Client Avatars (ICAs), audience segmentation, transformation language, Dream 100 lists, creator scouting matrices, or any worksheet that asks "who is this for and what do they actually want."

## Core principles

IEI's approach to audience is structurally opposite to most marketing playbooks. We do not start with broad demographics and narrow down. We start with **a specific person**, give them a name, and write everything to them. We then build status filters that **reject** misfits as aggressively as we attract fits. The community is "not inclusive" — it is a high-status environment where access is earned through alignment.

Every audience decision rests on five premises:

1. **Identity over transaction.** The brand is the foundational state; the audience is the citizenry. We are not selling a product to a consumer. We are inviting a stranger to join an identity.
2. **Pain over feature.** We define the audience by what's broken in their life right now (Island A), not by demographic descriptors. "32-year-old male, $80k income, lives in Brooklyn" is useless. "Tired of ad-spend roulette and watching his last agency burn $40k on stock-photo creative" is actionable.
3. **Status filter, not funnel.** We filter incoming attention by alignment with the Brand Soul, not just budget. A "Whale" lead with the wrong philosophy is still a Vampire.
4. **Information Gain is the hook.** What pulls the right audience in is the truth that nobody else is telling them. The audience self-identifies as "the kind of person who actually wants the receipts."
5. **A real Enemy is the loyalty engine.** The audience must share an enemy with the brand. "Us vs. Them" — vs. PR-spin, mass-produced fast fashion, generic agency-speak, parasitic discount sites, performative niceness. Without a shared enemy, the audience stays a market, never becomes a tribe.

## Frameworks

### The Ideal Client Avatar (ICA) — Naming Convention

Every brand playbook produces a single **named** ICA. Not a segment, not a persona cluster — a person with a first name. Examples used internally: **Jordan** (OffScript). Once named, the ICA is referenced by name in QA and creative reviews: *"If Jordan saw this, would he share it or scroll past because it feels like an ad?"*

The "Jordan Test" is one of three Brand Soul Stress-Tests applied to every output, alongside the "Enemy Test" (is this accidentally helping the Enemy by looking corporate?) and the "Information Gain Test" (does this provide a truth that isn't currently being told?).

### The "Island A → Island B" Transformation Map

The most distinctive audience tool in the IEI vocabulary. Every ICA exists on **Island A** (their current pain, frustration, and current reality) and is being recruited toward **Island B** (the desired outcome, identity, and future state). The brand is the ferry.

Both islands must be defined with specificity:

- **Island A — Current Pain.** Not feelings. Specific behaviors and circumstances. *"Spending $10k/mo on Meta ads with no creative refresh since Q1, watching CPM rise weekly, getting 'we're optimizing' updates from a junior media buyer."*
- **Island B — Desired Outcome.** Not aspirations. Specific identity and result. *"A brand operating like the Top 1%, where every $1 of ad spend buys $4 of revenue, and the founder gets out of the daily creative review loop."*

Every onboarding webhook, sales script, ad hook, and lead-magnet headline must explicitly tie back to a specific Island A pain. New community members are auto-tagged in "Starter Threads" based on the Island A pain identified in the sales process. Every reasoning step in IEI's agentic systems begins by identifying "the prospect's Island A (current pain) and Island B (desired outcome)."

When generating an ICA worksheet, never list Island B without listing the precise Island A pain point it cures. Generic "Island B" outcomes ("more growth," "better results") are rejected.

### The "Whale vs. Vampire" Matrix

The qualifying filter for every incoming lead. Replaces traditional lead scoring. Binary, brutal, and based on five dimensions:

| Feature | The Whale (Target) | The Vampire (Reject) |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $2M – $50M ARR | <$500k ARR |
| Ad Spend | Already $10k+/mo | "Looking to start" with $500 |
| Philosophy | Believes the Enemy logic | Wants "Safe/Corporate" marketing |
| Authority | Speaking to Founder/CMO | Speaking to a junior intern |
| Mindset | Seeking Strategic Partner | Seeking Fulfillment Vendor |

The Vampire is dangerous not because they have no money, but because they are **high-stress and low-margin**. They consume disproportionate operational capacity and pull the brand off-mission. Claude is forbidden from booking a sales call for a prospect that fails the matrix.

### The Lead Scoring Rubric (0–100)

Sits on top of the Whale/Vampire matrix and assigns a numeric score after the **Digital Footprint Audit** (an "Intelligence Sweep" of the prospect's Meta/TikTok ad library, search authority, brand soul presence, and tech stack):

- **85–100, Priority Whale** — Personal video from founder + 48-hour call booking.
- **60–84, Growth Potential** — Send a "Self-Service Audit" first; if completed, book a call.
- **<60, Tire-Kicker** — Automatic rejection email or referral to lower-tier provider, with the high-status disqualifier script: *"To be blunt, our [engine] would likely break your current fulfillment. We only partner with brands that have a 3:1 LTV/CAC ratio already proven. Focus on fixing [X] first, and let's talk in 6 months."*

### The 4-Stage Tribal Funnel

Every brand audience is organized into four progressive stages of commitment. The job of the brand is to systematically move people from Stage 1 to Stage 4.

1. **Stranger (The Observer)** — Consuming content on rented platforms (TikTok, IG Reels). No relationship, no data, owned by the algorithm.
2. **Spectator (The Lead)** — Entered the database via a low-friction lead magnet. We know their email, but they haven't paid.
3. **Member (The Citizen)** — Purchased the core offer, entered the "Owned Moat" (Discord, SMS list, paid community).
4. **Evangelist (The Soldier)** — Actively recruiting others and defending the brand against the Enemy. UGC creators, repeat buyers, leaderboard-climbers.

Each stage has a different content cadence, different information access, and different status. Public content is for Strangers and Spectators. Owned content (Discord, members-only) is for Members and Evangelists and must prioritize **Intimacy and Authority** over reach.

### The Creator/Partnership Scouting Matrix (Seed-to-Scale)

For brands that work with creators, IEI segments **partners** the same way it segments customers — through a 4-stage funnel with binary promotion gates:

| Stage | Name | Interaction | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | The Seeding | Product-only (gifting). No strings. | Vibe Check. Do they actually wear/use it? |
| 02 | The Pilot | First paid post or commission test. | Efficiency Check. CAC and CTR from their audience? |
| 03 | The Partner | 3–6 month contract. Retainer + commission. | Scaling. Integrate into Paid Ads OS (whitelisting). |
| 04 | The Ambassador | 12-month "Face of the Brand" deal. | Equity. Joins the "Brain Trust." |

A creator cannot move from Seeding to Partner without three Success Signals:
- **Signal A (Organic Reach):** Gifted post engagement rate ≥150% of their account average.
- **Signal B (Direct Conversion):** Pilot post ROAS ≥2.5x.
- **Signal C (Creative Utility):** Raw footage usable in ≥3 winning ad variations.

When ROAS drops below 1.5x for two consecutive months, the **Sunset Protocol** triggers: a "30-Day Rest" or a Clean Break with a graceful exit script.

### The Status Economy & Role Architecture

Inside the Owned Moat (Discord/Skool/custom community), the audience is governed by a **Status Economy**. Members do not stay for "features"; they stay to rise in the hierarchy.

- **Role Architecture:** Tiered roles defining expertise. Example progression: *Novice → Architect → Sovereign*.
- **Privilege Tiers:** High-level tiers get direct access to "Informed Sibling" and "Alpha" data before public release.
- **Social Proof Loop:** Automated "Win-Tracking" where member successes are broadcast to the Spectator layer to drive FOMO.
- **Peer-to-Peer Facilitation:** Architect-level members rewarded for helping Novice members. The community becomes a network effect, not a content channel.
- **Network Effect Logic:** A community becomes a Moat only when value is generated by Members, not just the Founder. Shared assets — collaborative wikis, member-contributed resource vaults — are the lock-in.

### Sanity vs. Vanity Audience Metrics

When evaluating audience health, ignore vanity metrics. IEI's audience metric matrix:

| Channel | Vanity (ignore) | Sanity (optimize for) | What it tells us |
|---|---|---|---|
| Short-form video | Total views | 3-second retention rate, share rate | Hook worked / hit emotional nerve |
| Static carousels | Likes | Saves, DM sends | Highly valuable / validating |
| Long-form (YouTube) | Subscriber count | CTR, AVD | Packaging / pacing worked |
| Conversion posts | Engagement rate | Link clicks, DM keyword triggers | Audience trusts brand enough to leave platform |
| Owned land (Discord/Email) | Total member count | DAU, reply rate | Living ecosystem vs. dead lurker graveyard |

Subscriber count and follower count are essentially noise. The real audience signal is **DAU**, **reply rate**, and **DMs that contain a keyword the brand can answer with a direct sale**.

### The Brand Identity Questionnaire (audience inputs)

The Blank Brand Identity Questionnaire feeds the persona engine. The audience-relevant inputs are:

- **Ideal Clientele or Audience** — Have you identified whom your ideal clientele is? Specifics, not segments.
- **Brand Personality Traits** — 3–5 traits that characterize the brand (e.g., innovative, trustworthy, adventurous). Used to filter audience match.
- **Brand Tone, Voice, and Persona** — Formal, informal, friendly, authoritative, mentor, friend, expert. Audience should feel "talked with," not "marketed to."
- **Value Proposition** — How does the brand solve audience pain points or fulfill needs? This is the Island A → Island B bridge in compressed form.
- **Competitors** — 3 competitors or brands/people the brand wishes to emulate. Used for Reconnaissance Hitlist (see below).

### The Reconnaissance Hitlist (audience research input)

To find the audience, study three categories of "Top 1%" accounts the audience is already paying attention to:

1. **Direct Rivals** — Brands fighting for the same wallet share. Goal: find weak spots; out-execute.
2. **Aspirational Giants** — Brands in the broader industry operating at 10x scale. Goal: study production value, community management, monetization funnels.
3. **Out-of-Niche Innovators** — Creators or brands in completely unrelated industries breaking the algorithm. Goal: steal cross-industry mechanics before competitors do.

For every "unicorn" piece of content the target audience is engaging with, run a **Content Autopsy** — Hook (0–3s psychological trigger), Pacing (visual rhythm), Aesthetic/Format (raw vs. produced), CTA (transition to ask), Comment Section (real pain points and desires of the audience).

The comment-section analysis is the most underrated signal. *"What is the audience actually talking about in the replies?"* That language is Island A in their own words — and should be reused verbatim in ad hooks, lead magnet titles, and cold outreach.

## Audience patterns

**The audience is named.** First name. The whole team uses it ("Would Jordan share this?"). Forces specificity in every creative decision.

**Pain is described in specifics, not feelings.** Not "frustrated with marketing." Instead: *"watching CAC climb 18% in two quarters while their last agency keeps sending Loom recaps that don't address the creative refresh"*. Feelings come from circumstances; the circumstances are what make the avatar feel seen.

**Demographics are last, not first.** Age, income, geography appear at the bottom of the worksheet, not the top. They're a sanity check after the psychographics and behaviors are locked.

**Behavioral signals beat declarative ones.** What the audience *does* (saves carousels, replies to emails, follows three specific competitors, browses Reddit threads on a niche topic) is more diagnostic than what they say they want.

**The Enemy is named.** Every audience definition should include "what is the audience exhausted by, lied to by, or in mild rebellion against?" This is the bonding agent.

**The audience has a vocabulary.** Borrow it. The "secret handshake" Green-Light Words (Archive, Receipts, Deconstructed, Heavyweight, Signal-to-noise, Deadstock) come from the audience first, not the brand. The brand earns trust by using the audience's own language correctly.

**The audience is segmented by stage, not segment.** Stranger / Spectator / Member / Evangelist — not "Cluster A / Cluster B / Cluster C." Stage tells you what content they need next; segments don't.

**The audience can be sized.** Calculate: how many Strangers do we need to produce one Member at current conversion rates? How many Members to one Evangelist? If the math doesn't pencil to a sustainable LTV:CAC of 3:1+, the audience definition is wrong, not the funnel.

**Status filter, always on.** It is more valuable to **reject** misfits than to broaden to capture them. Every public-facing line should subtly disqualify the wrong people. *"If that sounds like your speed, the link's in bio. If not, no hard feelings."*

## Anti-patterns to avoid

**Generic demographic personas.** "Sarah, 32, marketing manager, loves yoga and travel." Useless. Reject any persona that could fit five different brands.

**Adjective-only descriptions.** "Discerning, premium, sophisticated, intentional." These don't describe a person; they describe how marketers see themselves. Replace with behaviors.

**Inclusive-by-default language.** "For everyone who values quality." If the audience is "everyone," there is no audience. Sharpen until 50% of readers feel "this is not for me" — that's how the other 50% feel chosen.

**Aspirational without origin.** Listing Island B without Island A. Every "we help you become X" must answer "...because right now you're stuck doing Y."

**Vampire-inclusion.** Designing the audience to capture the largest possible pool. The 3:1 LTV:CAC threshold is non-negotiable. If a segment is under it, kill the segment.

**Creator-segment confusion.** Treating creators as audience members. Creators are a separate "Seed-to-Scale" pipeline with their own gates. Conflating the two produces incoherent partnership decisions.

**Treating community as a marketing channel.** The community is the **product**, not the megaphone. If community content is just repackaged public marketing, members churn within 30 days for "Information Overload" or "Lack of Status." The Recursive Moat Audit checks for this every 30 days.

**Vanity-metric thinking.** "We have 50k followers." Worthless if the DAU on the Discord is 12 and the reply rate on the email list is 0.4%. Always pivot reporting to Sanity Metrics.

**One-size content cadence.** Public posts to Strangers and members-only Deep-Dive Labs cannot have the same tone, depth, or frequency. The 4-Stage Tribal Funnel demands different content per stage.

**Ignoring the comment section.** The audience is telling you exactly what they want, in their own language, in the replies on competitors' viral posts. Skipping that scrape and starting from internal brainstorm is malpractice.

## Worked example

**Brief:** Define the audience for a hypothetical media-literacy subscription product (AceTV+ style).

**Weak version (generic):**
> "Our target audience is media-savvy adults aged 25–45 with a household income of $75k+ who are interested in current events, value transparency, and want to think critically about the news. Primarily urban and suburban, college-educated, active on social media."

Why it fails: Could describe the audience for the New York Times, NPR, or any centrist news brand. No Enemy. No specific behaviors. No Island A pain. No vocabulary clue. The brand could be anyone.

**IEI version:**

> **The ICA: "Maya."** 29, works in tech-adjacent operations, used to read three newsletters and now reads zero because every one of them turned into a sponsored-content vehicle for the same five brands. Cancelled her NYT subscription after the third "explainer" she could tell was a press-release re-write. Watches a lot of YouTube video essays at 1.5x speed. Saves carousels she'd "never share but want to remember." Follows two creators who do politics-adjacent media criticism and lurks in their Discords without posting. Has tried to explain to her family group chat why a specific cable-news segment was misleading and has given up. Owns a Kindle. Will pay for things that respect her time.
>
> **Island A (current pain):**
> - Cannot trust mainstream summaries — every "explainer" reads like PR-spin.
> - Spending 40+ minutes a day on news consumption with declining confidence she actually knows what's happening.
> - Has muted three former-favorite podcasts because they took on advertisers she finds untrustworthy.
> - Has no peer group that meets her level of skepticism without tipping into conspiracy.
>
> **Island B (desired outcome):**
> - 15 minutes a day, signal-only, with receipts.
> - A Discord of peers who can discuss nuance without performing it.
> - The status of being the friend who actually read the report.
>
> **The Enemy:** Mainstream PR spin, sponsored-content media literacy theater, algorithmic echo chambers, podcast networks that took the money.
>
> **Vocabulary (Green-Light, scraped from comment sections of two adjacent creators):** receipts, signal-to-noise, deep-dive, deconstructed, manufacturing consent, the actual paper, source check, the bias underneath.
>
> **Whale-or-Vampire fit:** Whale at $20–30/mo. Vampires are the "I want it free" Reddit cohort and the "give me hot takes" Twitter cohort — both filtered out of paid acquisition.
>
> **Stage entry point:** Spectator via the lead magnet "10-Point Bias-Detection Checklist (PDF)." Promoted to Member at first paid month. Promoted to Evangelist when she invites two friends to the Discord and posts at least once.

Why it works: Maya is a person, not a segment. Her behaviors are specific, observable, and actionable for a copywriter. Her vocabulary is borrowed from her own posting/commenting patterns. Her Enemy is specific. She's filtered against Vampires before a single ad runs. Her stage progression is explicit. Every future creative decision can be tested against "Would Maya share this, or scroll past?"
