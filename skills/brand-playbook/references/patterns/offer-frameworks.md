# Offer & Monetization Patterns

> Distilled from 9 IEI training documents. Use this when generating offer architecture, pricing, value stacks, proposals, retention funnels, launch sequencing, or any worksheet that asks "what is the brand actually selling and how is it stacked."

## Core principles

IEI does not sell commodities. The brand is the **only cure** for a specific problem the audience is exhausted by. Every offer is constructed to feel inevitable — once the diagnostic has landed, the prescription is the only logical next move. Five rules govern every offer the brand builds:

1. **Cure, not commodity.** Position the offer as a prescription for a specific pain, not a product in a category. A category invites comparison-shopping. A cure does not. *"You don't price-shop the only doctor who can fix the thing."*
2. **Diagnose before you prescribe.** Sales is medicine, not pitching. The offer is presented after the audit has surfaced the leaks and quantified the dollar cost of inaction. Pricing without diagnosis is begging.
3. **Value-based, never hourly.** Price is anchored to the outcome (Island A → Island B), not the inputs (hours, deliverables). Hours are how amateurs price; outcomes are how partners price.
4. **EBITDA over clout.** Every growth strategy must be auditable for profitability. We do not scale for "clout"; we scale for Net Margin. If a campaign would break gross margin <50%, we don't run it.
5. **The offer is a system, not a SKU.** The product is upstream of a Lifecycle Funnel, a Retention Engine, and a Status Economy. A standalone product without a follow-on architecture is a leaky bucket.

## Frameworks

### "Cure Not Commodity" — Offer Engineering

The umbrella frame. Every offer must answer four questions before pricing is written:

- **What specific pain does this cure?** Named in the audience's own language. Not "marketing inefficiency" — *"watching your ad account hemorrhage $7k/mo on creative refreshes that never happen."*
- **Who is currently selling a worse version of the cure?** The Enemy is named. Their solution is described and broken down on the same page.
- **What is the receipt that this cure works?** Specific data, named clients, before/after audit numbers, GSM tests, ROAS lifts. Vague claims kill conversion.
- **Who is this not for?** Explicit disqualification. *"If you're under $500k ARR, this will break your fulfillment."* This raises status and conversion simultaneously.

When generating an offer, never write the price first. Write these four answers first. Pricing emerges from them.

### The "Prescription" Framework for Offers

The structural template that turns an audit into a sale. Six components, in order:

1. **The Diagnosis** — What is broken right now, in specifics. Quoted from the prospect's own words during discovery, plus hard data from the Digital Footprint Audit.
2. **The Cost of Inaction** — Specific dollar value of the leak. *"This single broken pixel is leaking ~$4,200/month in mis-attributed conversions."* Quantified, not vague.
3. **The Prescription** — The specific protocol, named like a treatment plan, not a service tier. *"The 90-Day Creative Refresh Engine,"* not "Premium Marketing Package."
4. **The Mechanism of Action** — Why this specific protocol works on this specific pain. References the underlying playbook logic. Demystifies, but doesn't give away the IP.
5. **The First Win** — The low-hanging fruit executed in week one to prove value before the contract has even been fully invoiced. Trust is built through speed.
6. **The Risk Reversal** — Outcome-based confidence: a Work-for-Free trigger, a Performance Kicker, or a defined "if we don't hit X by day 30, we extend Y."

The Prescription is presented in the **Technical Audit Deck** — a visual document that holds the prospect's "Current Reality" against the "Playbook Standard" line by line. The Audit Deck is the moment the sale closes; the proposal afterward is administrative.

### The Technical Audit Deck (the Visual Kill-Shot)

The Audit Deck is structured as **"Current Reality vs. The Playbook Standard"** — a two-column comparison where every "Leak" is paired with a Fix and a specific dollar value of the loss.

Section examples:
| Section | The "Leak" (Current Reality) | The "Fix" (Playbook Standard) |
|---|---|---|
| Brand Soul | Generic "stock" feel. No Mascot. | The Informed Sibling Persona. |
| Paid Ads | High CPC, low Creative Diversification. | The 1-of-1 Creative Engine. |
| Retention | Missing Cart Abandonment / Win-back flows. | The Profit Recovery Flow. |
| Search | Ranking for ego keywords only. | Commercial Intent Clusters. |

Slide types inside the deck:
- **The Creative Autopsy** — Screenshot of the prospect's current best-performing ad with redline annotations: *"This hook is too slow. This CTA is generic. This fails the Mascot test."*
- **The Search Gap Map** — Visual comparison of the prospect's rankings vs. a competitor using better SEO tactics.
- **The Lifecycle Leak Chart** — Diagram showing where users fall out of the funnel and the dollar amount lost at each stage.
- **The "Enemy" Comparison Slide** — *"What they tell you"* (e.g., *"We're optimizing for ROAS"*) vs. *"What we do"* (*"We optimize for EBITDA"*).
- **The "First 30 Days" Visual Roadmap** — Week 1: First Win. Week 2: Creative Engine Ignition. Week 4: First Drop or Cluster Launch.

Every Leak gets a specific dollar value. Vague urgency is rejected.

### The Universal Business Model Modifier

The set of dimensions every offer should be consciously varied across. When generating a brand's offer architecture, walk each dimension explicitly — defaulting in any of these is how generic offers happen.

- **Tier Structure.** Single offer vs. tiered ladder vs. bundled bundle. IEI default: a tiered ladder (Starter / Core / Empire) where Tier 3 is the one we actually want sold.
- **Cadence.** One-time vs. recurring vs. usage-based vs. drop-based. Drop-based mechanics manufacture scarcity that subscriptions cannot.
- **Access Layer.** Public vs. members-only vs. invite-only. Invite-only converts differently than public — pricing can move 3–5x for the same underlying deliverable.
- **Risk Position.** Buyer carries the risk vs. seller carries it. Performance kickers, work-for-free triggers, and outcome-based pricing shift risk to the seller and command higher prices.
- **Time-to-Value.** Days vs. weeks vs. months. The First Win Protocol pulls perceived time-to-value to <7 days even on long contracts.
- **Status Currency.** What does buying confer beyond the deliverable? Founder access, archive access, leaderboard placement, Ambassador roles.
- **Exclusivity.** Open enrollment vs. capped cohort vs. one client per category. Capped cohorts ("we only take 2 clients per quarter") are an anchor for high-ticket pricing.
- **Distribution.** Direct vs. partner vs. affiliate. The Affiliate Ascension Ladder is its own pricing logic (see below).

A complete offer specifies a position on every dimension consciously.

### The Three-Tier Choice Architecture

Default offer structure for high-ticket. Three options, designed so the **middle is the obvious answer**:

- **Tier 1 — The Starter ("Decoy")**: Stripped-back. Only the diagnostic deliverable, none of the system. Priced to make Tier 2 feel inevitable.
- **Tier 2 — The Core ("Target")**: The full Prescription as designed. The version the brand actually wants sold. Anchored against Tier 3.
- **Tier 3 — The Empire ("Anchor")**: All-in: full system + founder access + custom build-out + Ambassador-tier perks. Priced ~2.5x Tier 2. Some buyers take it; most use it as the anchor that makes Tier 2 a bargain.

Strict guardrail (from sales playbook): **No discounting**. If a buyer pushes back on Tier 2 price, Claude is forbidden from offering Tier 1 as a discount. Instead, Claude re-anchors to the Cost of Inaction quantified in the Diagnosis. *"You aren't comparing $25k to $0. You're comparing $25k to the $48k you'll burn next quarter on the current trajectory."*

### Risk Reversal — Outcome-Based Confidence

Three patterns for shifting risk to the seller (which is also why the seller can charge more):

- **The Work-for-Free Trigger** — *"If we don't hit [specific metric] by day 30, the next month is on us."*
- **The Performance Kicker** — Lower base + percentage of upside. Common for paid media engagements where the agency takes a cut of revenue lifted above a baseline.
- **The Cancellation Carve-Out** — *"You can cancel anytime after the first 90 days, no fee, no friction. We don't lock people in; we earn the renewal."*

Risk Reversals must be **specific and measurable**. "We guarantee satisfaction" is meaningless. "We guarantee a 2.5x ROAS by day 60 or we refund the management fee" is real.

### The Lifecycle / Retention Funnel Architecture

The offer is upstream of a Lifecycle Engine. Without retention architecture, even great acquisition burns cash. The architecture has standardized components:

- **The Welcome Vault** — Within 24 hours of purchase, the buyer gets access to a "Client HQ" (Notion/ClickUp board) housing the playbook and a personal Success Roadmap. Not an email — a full environment.
- **The Orientation Loom** — 2-minute founder video explaining what happens next.
- **The Success Gift** — Automated Zapier/Printful trigger sending a physical brand item. Shifts relationship from Vendor to Partner.
- **The First Win Protocol** — Specific high-impact task delivered in the first 7 days. For paid ads: fix a broken pixel or launch a quick-win retargeting flow. For SEO: refresh the highest-intent page. For lifecycle: set up the cart-abandonment flow they were missing.
- **The 120-Day Sunset Logic** — When a user hits 120 days of zero opens/clicks, schedule the Sunset Sequence. Email 1 is plain text: *"Hey, it's [Founder]. Notice you haven't been opening these. If you want to stay on the list, click here. If not, no stress."* Move non-clickers to Suppressed (don't delete; preserve historical data; never email again).
- **Frequency Capping.** Email: 1–2x/week baseline, up to 4x in a 7-day Launch Surge. SMS: 2–4x/month baseline, up to 3x during a launch.
- **The Reply Hack** — Specific [GIVE] emails scripted to trigger direct replies. *"Reply to this with your favorite drop so far and Ryan will pick one to get early access."* User replies = strongest deliverability signal.
- **Compliance constraints baked in** — Every SMS ends with "Reply STOP to opt out." Quiet hours: 9am–8pm local. Email: physical address, unsubscribe link, "why you're receiving this."

### The Ad Campaign Funnel Architecture (Three-Stage Flywheel)

For monetization through paid acquisition, every piece of content/ad gets a defined Traffic Role. Posts without a role are killed.

- **Stage 1 — The Rented Land (Fishing)** — Content for algorithmic reach. AI Rule: 80% value/entertainment, 20% soft CTA to profile.
- **Stage 2 — The Bridge (Funneling)** — Content that captures attention from Stage 1. Link-in-bio, lead magnets, Story sequences, DM automation triggers (*"Comment 'DROP' to get the secret link"*).
- **Stage 3 — The Owned Land (Monetizing & Retaining)** — Content for the inner circle. Discord exclusives, SMS drops, email newsletters, ticketed events.

Every offer launch must allocate spend and creative across all three stages, not just Stage 1.

### The Launch & Drop Sequencer (The Hype Machine)

For drop-based offers (1-of-1s, limited runs, cohort launches). Manufactured scarcity and urgency, sequenced across rented and owned channels:

- **T-14 days — The Tease.** Soft visual on Stories/Reels. No commercial intent. Build curiosity, not urgency.
- **T-7 days — The Reveal.** Mascot/founder video on the product. Email goes to full list. Spectators added to a waitlist (low-friction form).
- **T-3 days — The Proof.** Creator content drops on rented land. UGC and "behind-the-build" footage. Discord gets a private preview thread.
- **T-1 day — The Pre-Drop.** SMS surge to opted-in subscribers. Final creator content cycle. Story countdown stickers.
- **T-0 — The Drop.** Owned-land first (Discord/email), public second. Spike of organic interest is the algorithmic signal.
- **T+1 hour — Sold-Out / Re-stock signal.** Hard scarcity confirmed. Email to non-buyers: *"Sold out in 47 minutes. Here's the waitlist for the next archive piece."*
- **Launch Sprint mechanics** — *"First creator to hit 20 sales on Drop 02 gets a $500 bonus + a 1-of-1 'Founder Edition' hoodie."* Gamified leaderboard in the creator Discord.

Drop logic is what turns a streetwear brand into a status economy. The constraint is the product.

### The Affiliate Ascension Ladder

For brands monetizing through creators/affiliates. Three tiers, with promotion gated by sales volume:

- **Tier 1 — The Rookie (0–10 sales):** 10% commission on net sales.
- **Tier 2 — The Pro (11–50 sales):** 15% commission + free early access to next 1-of-1 drop.
- **Tier 3 — The Partner (50+ sales):** 20% commission + a "Creator-Exclusive" product they help design.

Operational guardrails:
- **Net-30 Rule** — Payouts 30 days after the sale (returns/chargebacks).
- **Weekly Transparency Report** — Friday DM: *"Yo [Name], you drove 14 sales this week ($210 commission). The 'Boxy Tee' is your highest converter. Lean into that angle."*
- **No-Coupon-Site Clause** — Strict ban on submitting codes to Honey/RetailMeNot. Submission = instant termination. Protects margin.
- **Tracking** — Dual-track (Vanity Code like *"OFFSCRIPT_JORDAN"* + Dynamic UTM `?utm_source=creator_name`). 30-day cookie window.

### Value-Based Proposal Logic

Replaces "hourly rates" and "fixed retainers" with outcome anchoring. Three components:

1. **The Outcome Anchor.** Specific dollar value the buyer will gain or save in 90/180 days. Backed by the Audit Deck data.
2. **The Multiplier Math.** *"This $25k/mo retainer is anchored to a $300k+ EBITDA lift over 12 months — a 12x multiple on agency fees."*
3. **The Comparison Frame.** Side-by-side cost of (a) doing nothing, (b) hiring a junior in-house team, (c) the IEI engagement. The IEI engagement is structured to be the obvious efficient option, not the "premium" option.

Proposal anti-patterns: line-item lists of "deliverables." Hours estimates. "Up to 5 ad creatives per week" framing. These all reframe the buyer as someone purchasing inputs. The buyer is purchasing an outcome.

### Pricing Logic — Margin Benchmarks

Internal financial guardrails Claude enforces when sizing offers:

- **Gross Margin target:** 60–70%.
- **Net Profit Margin target:** 20–35%.
- **LTV/CAC minimum:** 3:1.

If a proposed offer would push gross margin below 50%, the offer is restructured — usually by stripping deliverables from Tier 2 and pushing them to Tier 3, or by introducing a Performance Kicker that makes the high-cost deliverable usage-based rather than included.

## Offer patterns

**Name the protocol.** Offers are named like treatments, not packages. *"The 90-Day Creative Refresh Engine,"* *"The Profit Recovery Flow,"* *"The Skyscraper Takeover SOP,"* *"The 1-of-1 Drop Engine."* "Premium Plan / Pro Plan / Enterprise Plan" is forbidden — it signals commodity.

**Quantify everything.** Every leak gets a dollar value. Every outcome gets a metric. Every claim gets a receipt. Vague offers get rejected — internally and by buyers.

**Anchor against inaction, not competitors.** The price comparison the buyer should make is "this offer vs. doing nothing for another 90 days." Comparing to competitors invites discount thinking.

**Capped capacity is a feature.** *"We only take 2 clients per quarter."* This is real, not theatrical — it protects quality, and it transparently sets the price floor.

**The First Win in week one.** Every offer ships a tangible win in the first 7 days, before the second invoice cycles. Front-load value to reduce churn risk in the 0–30 day window.

**The Status Gift.** Physical artifact in week one. Hoodie, deck, archive piece. Shifts the relationship from transaction to membership.

**Scarcity is the mechanic, not the marketing.** Drop quantities, capped cohorts, timed launches. The constraint is real. *"We made 200. When they're gone, they're gone — no restock, no email asking you to wait nicely."*

**The cancellation door is open.** Lock-ins are status leaks. Long contracts with friction-free cancellation outperform shorter contracts with retention friction, because they shift the burden of value-proof onto the seller.

**Margin is sacred.** Below 50% gross margin, the offer is wrong. Either re-tier, add a kicker, or kill the deliverable. We do not "scale into bankruptcy."

## Anti-patterns to avoid

**Hourly billing.** Hours are inputs. Buyers don't want inputs. Banned.

**Generic tiered names.** "Basic / Pro / Enterprise" or "Bronze / Silver / Gold." Lazy, commodity-coded, and they invite price-shopping. Always name the protocol.

**Discount-to-close.** The fastest way to destroy positioning. If a buyer can't afford Tier 2, the answer is "you're not ready for this engagement, here's what to fix first" — not "let me knock $5k off."

**Bundling without strategic logic.** Throwing in "bonuses" to inflate perceived value. Insults the buyer's intelligence. Each component of the offer must connect to the Prescription.

**Vanity offers.** Building products to win awards or impress peers rather than cure pain. The EBITDA Mandate kills these before launch.

**Set-and-forget post-purchase.** Selling the offer and assuming retention will happen. The Lifecycle Funnel must be ready before the ads turn on. The Master Architect rule: *"Is the Post-Purchase Flow ready before we turn on the ads?"*

**Generic launch sequences.** Same email cadence regardless of audience temperature. The Drop Sequencer's value is differentiation by stage and channel.

**Ignoring frequency caps.** Sending 5 emails a week to maximize a single launch. Burns the list. Every campaign respects the baseline cadence unless a defined Launch Surge is authorized.

**Affiliate margin leaks.** Letting creators submit codes to coupon sites. A single Honey leak can compress margin across an entire cohort. Enforce the No-Coupon-Site clause and terminate violators.

**Risk reversals that aren't measurable.** "Satisfaction guaranteed" is a non-statement. If the Risk Reversal can't be triggered by a specific metric, it's marketing copy, not a guarantee.

**Pitching before diagnosing.** Sending a proposal without an Audit Deck. The proposal will lose to whichever competitor did do the audit, regardless of price.

## Worked example

**Brief:** Architect a high-ticket offer for an agency selling paid-ads management to DTC streetwear brands.

**Weak version (commodity):**
> "**Premium Paid Ads Management — $5k/month**
> - Up to 10 ad creatives per month
> - Daily ad account management
> - Weekly reporting calls
> - Dedicated account manager
> - Custom reporting dashboard
>
> Sign up today and get your first month free!"

Why it fails: Every line is an input. No diagnosis. No specific cure. Discount-to-close ("first month free"). Generic title. No risk reversal. Compares directly with every other agency — invites a 30% price cut.

**IEI version:**

> **The 1-of-1 Creative Engine — A 90-Day Prescription for Brands Burning Cash on Stale Ad Accounts.**
>
> **The Diagnosis (from your Audit Deck):**
> Your last 90 days of Meta ads ran 7 unique creatives. Average CPM has climbed 34%. Top performer is 11 weeks old. Your competitor (Brand X) is shipping 22 creatives a month and pushing CPMs in your category 18% higher than they were a year ago. You're losing the bid auction every Tuesday and Thursday.
>
> **The Cost of Inaction:** ~$11,400/month in inflated CAC at current burn. ~$140,000 over the next 12 months if nothing changes.
>
> **The Prescription:** The 1-of-1 Creative Engine. We ship 20 net-new ad creatives per month, briefed against your specific audience's Island A pain, sourced from a vetted UGC creator pool, edited to platform-native specs, and refreshed every 14 days against the 3-Strike Pivot Logic.
>
> **The Mechanism of Action:** Most agencies optimize for ROAS at the campaign level. We optimize for **EBITDA** at the brand level — which means we ship more creative, kill underperformers faster, and feed winners into the Whitelisting layer of your Paid Ads OS. Your CAC drops because your creative pool is fresh, not because you've out-bid anyone.
>
> **The First Win (Week One):** We rebuild your retargeting audience structure and ship 5 hooks against the highest-intent segment. Historical baseline: ~$3,200/month in recovered cart abandonment alone, in the first 14 days.
>
> **The Status Gift:** OffScript-style heavyweight tee in your office Monday morning.
>
> **The Three Tiers:**
> - **Diagnostic Only — $4,500 one-time.** The Audit Deck delivered as a deliverable. (Most buyers use this only as a proof-of-fit.)
> - **The 1-of-1 Engine — $18,500/month.** The full Prescription. (90-day initial term, then month-to-month, cancel anytime.)
> - **The Founder's Quarter — $52,000/quarter.** Engine + Founder access + custom Ambassador-tier creator pool + bespoke Drop Sequencer for one launch. Capped at 2 clients per quarter.
>
> **The Risk Reversal:** If we don't hit a 2.5x blended ROAS by day 60, the management fee for month 3 is on us. Tracked in your Meta and Shopify dashboards — no math you can't verify yourself.
>
> **The Disqualification:** This isn't for brands under $500k ARR or under $10k/mo current ad spend — the engine will overwhelm your fulfillment. If that's you, we'll send a referral.

Why it works: Named protocol, not a tier. Diagnosis with specifics. Cost of Inaction quantified. Prescription tied to a named playbook (Paid Ads OS, Whitelisting, 3-Strike Pivot Logic). First Win on the table in week one. Three tiers with a decoy and an anchor. Risk Reversal is specific and verifiable. Capacity capped. Disqualification raises status. The buyer is choosing between this and "another quarter of $11,400/mo bleeding," not between this and a competing agency's $5k/mo package.
