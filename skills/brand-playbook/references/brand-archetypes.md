# Brand Archetypes Reference

> Load this file during **WS2 Brand Identity** to identify the brand's primary and secondary archetype.
> Apply throughout: voice (WS3), visual direction (Step 2 Brand Kit), content strategy, and social media planning.

---

## What Brand Archetypes Are

Brand archetypes are the 12 universal character patterns derived from Carl Jung's theory of the collective unconscious. They represent recurring roles and motivations that humans instantly recognize — not because they're learned, but because they're hardwired into how we process story and meaning.

When a brand owns an archetype, it stops being a product category and starts being a *character* people feel something about. Apple is The Creator. Harley-Davidson is The Outlaw. Nike is The Hero. Dove is The Caregiver. This is why their content works: every post, every campaign, every caption is the same character speaking.

**Without an archetype, a brand has no consistent voice — only tactics.**

## The Archetype Identification Process

### Step 1: Map the Brand's Core Drive

Ask: *What is this brand fundamentally trying to do in the world?*

| Drive | Points Toward |
|-------|--------------|
| Protect, serve, nurture | Caregiver |
| Achieve, win, prove | Hero |
| Disrupt, rebel, challenge | Outlaw |
| Connect, love, attract | Lover |
| Discover, explore, roam | Explorer |
| Create, build, imagine | Creator |
| Lead, control, organize | Ruler |
| Transform, inspire, change | Magician |
| Teach, analyze, inform | Sage |
| Belong, include, relate | Everyman |
| Delight, play, entertain | Jester |
| Simplify, optimism, purify | Innocent |

### Step 2: Identify the ICA's Core Desire

The archetype must match what the ideal client *wants to feel*, not just what the brand does.

| ICA Desire | Best Archetype Match |
|-----------|---------------------|
| "I want to feel powerful / accomplished" | Hero, Ruler |
| "I want to feel safe / cared for" | Caregiver, Innocent |
| "I want to feel special / desired" | Lover, Creator |
| "I want to feel free / adventurous" | Explorer, Outlaw |
| "I want to feel smart / respected" | Sage, Ruler |
| "I want to feel like I belong" | Everyman, Caregiver |
| "I want to feel inspired / transformed" | Magician, Creator |
| "I want to feel joyful / light" | Jester, Innocent |

### Step 3: Select Primary + Secondary Archetype

- **Primary**: The dominant character. 80% of all brand communication.
- **Secondary**: The supporting trait that adds dimension. Prevents the primary from becoming a cliché.
- Never mix more than two — a brand that's everything is nothing.

**Valid pairings and their brand personality result:**

| Primary | Secondary | Result |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Hero | Outlaw | Bold disruptor who fights and wins |
| Hero | Sage | Expert authority who educates and leads |
| Ruler | Sage | Prestigious institution with proven knowledge |
| Ruler | Creator | Established authority with innovative edge |
| Magician | Creator | Visionary who builds transformative experiences |
| Magician | Lover | Enchanting, seductive, transformative intimacy |
| Caregiver | Innocent | Wholesome, nurturing, trustworthy warmth |
| Caregiver | Everyman | Accessible, community-driven, practical care |
| Explorer | Outlaw | Rule-breaking adventurer who charts their own path |
| Explorer | Creator | Curious maker who discovers through building |
| Lover | Creator | Aesthetic, sensory, artisanal passion |
| Lover | Caregiver | Intimate, devoted, relationship-first brand |
| Sage | Creator | Thought leader who teaches through making |
| Jester + anything | | Jester must always be primary — it overpowers secondaries |
| Innocent + anything | | Works best paired with Caregiver or Everyman only |

## The 12 Archetypes — Full Profiles

---

### 01. THE INNOCENT

**Core desire:** Safety, happiness, simplicity, purity  
**Core fear:** Doing something wrong, becoming corrupt, punishment  
**Brand promise:** "We make things simple, safe, and good."  
**Superpower:** Optimism — this brand finds the good in everything  
**Shadow side:** Naivety, denial, inability to handle complexity

**Brand Examples:** Dove, Coca-Cola (classic era), Nintendo, Honest Company, Aveeno, Airbnb (early era)

**Voice + Tone:**
- Warm, gentle, optimistic, sincere
- Simple sentences. No jargon. No complexity.
- Uses words like: pure, simple, natural, honest, wholesome, joy, happy, clean, light
- Never edgy, never aggressive, never ironic
- Speaks to the best in people — no fear-based messaging

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- "Before & After" (simple problem → simple solution)
- "The simple truth about [topic]"
- Nostalgia and childhood memory
- Nature and clean living imagery
- Everyday moments made magical
- Feel-good storytelling with no conflict
- Transparency content ("Here's what's in it / how we make it")

**Social Voice:**
- Captions are short, light, uplifting
- Lots of natural light photography, soft palettes
- Avoid: sarcasm, dark humor, complexity, controversy
- Post timing: morning (optimism energy), weekend family moments

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Whites, soft pastels, sky blue, gentle yellow, soft green, peach
- **Avoid:** Black, heavy saturation, dark fields, high contrast
- **Typography:** Round, approachable serifs or clean humanist sans-serifs. Nothing sharp.
- **Imagery:** Natural light, outdoors, children, families, simplicity, clean spaces
- **Texture:** None or very subtle — purity means clean surfaces

**Font Pairings:**
- Playfair Display (heading) + Lato (body) + Nunito (accent)
- Merriweather (heading) + Source Sans Pro (body) + Quicksand (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Soft white (#FAFAF7) or pale sky (#EBF4FA)
- Accent: Gentle yellow (#F5E6A3) or sage green (#C8DDB4)
- Text: Warm charcoal (#3D3D3D) — never pure black

### 02. THE EVERYMAN (Regular Guy / Gal)

**Core desire:** Belonging, connection, equality, fitting in  
**Core fear:** Standing out, being seen as elitist, rejection from the group  
**Brand promise:** "We're just like you — and we've got what you need."  
**Superpower:** Relatability — this brand makes everyone feel seen  
**Shadow side:** Blandness, lowest-common-denominator, fear of differentiation

**Brand Examples:** IKEA, Target, Levi's, Home Depot, LinkedIn (professional era), Budweiser, Walmart

**Voice + Tone:**
- Conversational, direct, unpretentious, warm
- Talks like a trusted friend, not a brand
- Uses words like: real, everyday, honest, straightforward, practical, together, community, folks
- Self-deprecating humor works here
- Never condescending, never exclusive, never fancy

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Real people, real results (UGC-heavy)
- "You're not alone in this" empathy content
- Behind-the-scenes normalcy
- Community spotlights / customer stories
- "Here's the honest truth about [topic]" transparency
- Problem-solution with practical language
- Celebration of ordinary life done well

**Social Voice:**
- Conversational captions, like texting a friend
- Real photography over polished studio shots
- Memes and relatable humor perform well
- Community engagement (reply to every comment)
- Avoid: aspirational excess, luxury signals, corporate speak

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Navy, brick red, warm tan, forest green, denim blue, classic white
- **Avoid:** Excess gold, ultra-minimalism, anything that reads as "exclusive"
- **Typography:** Strong, legible workhorses. No decorative flourish.
- **Imagery:** Real people in real settings. Diverse. Unposed. Candid.
- **Texture:** Denim, wood grain, concrete — tactile, real, unpretentious

**Font Pairings:**
- Roboto Slab (heading) + Open Sans (body) + Roboto Mono (accent)
- Raleway (heading) + Source Sans Pro (body) + IBM Plex Sans (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Classic navy (#1B2A4A) or brick red (#B84A2E)
- Secondary: Warm tan (#C9A96E) or forest (#2D5A27)
- Background: Off-white (#F5F0E8) or clean white
- Accent: Denim blue or warm amber

### 03. THE HERO

**Core desire:** Mastery, achievement, courage, proving worth through action  
**Core fear:** Weakness, failure, cowardice, being shown as inadequate  
**Brand promise:** "We help you be the best version of yourself."  
**Superpower:** Courage — this brand confronts difficulty and wins  
**Shadow side:** Arrogance, recklessness, defining self only by achievement

**Brand Examples:** Nike, Adidas, Army, FedEx, Duracell, LinkedIn (career growth), Under Armour

**Voice + Tone:**
- Motivational, direct, confident, action-oriented
- Short punchy sentences. Commands. Present tense.
- Uses words like: win, achieve, push, conquer, transform, rise, dominate, relentless, earn
- Challenge the audience — "you can do this if you want it bad enough"
- Never apologetic, never soft, never vague

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Challenge / transformation content ("30-day challenge")
- Success stories with struggle-to-triumph arc
- "What separates good from great" thought leadership
- Performance tips, optimization, elite habits
- Behind the scenes of achieving something hard
- "Most people won't do this" — exclusivity of effort
- Urgency-based CTAs

**Social Voice:**
- Bold, punchy captions — 1–3 lines max
- High contrast photography: action shots, strong lighting
- Reels/video of people in motion, achieving
- Motivational quote graphics perform well
- Avoid: victimhood framing, softness, too much nuance

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Black, white, red, electric blue, deep charcoal, gold (earned, not luxe)
- **Avoid:** Pastels, soft curves, anything fragile-looking
- **Typography:** Bold geometric sans-serifs. High contrast. No delicate fonts.
- **Imagery:** Action, motion, sweat, achievement, strong bodies, dynamic angles
- **Texture:** Carbon fiber, metal, concrete — materials of performance

**Font Pairings:**
- Bebas Neue (heading) + Work Sans (body) + DM Sans (accent)
- Barlow Condensed (heading) + Inter (body) + Space Mono (accent)
- Oswald (heading) + Roboto (body) + Roboto Mono (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Near-black (#111111) or deep charcoal (#1C1C1E)
- Accent: Nike red (#E03A3E) or electric blue (#0057FF)
- Secondary: White or light grey for contrast
- Text: White on dark, near-black on light

### 04. THE OUTLAW (Rebel / Revolutionary)

**Core desire:** Revolution, disruption, breaking rules, radical freedom  
**Core fear:** Powerlessness, conformity, being just like everyone else  
**Brand promise:** "We break what's broken and replace it with something better."  
**Superpower:** Disruption — this brand identifies the enemy and destroys it  
**Shadow side:** Destructiveness for its own sake, alienating potential allies

**Brand Examples:** Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel, Red Bull, Dollar Shave Club, Patagonia, Supreme

**Voice + Tone:**
- Raw, direct, provocative, irreverent
- Calls out the status quo. Names the enemy.
- Uses words like: screw, real, raw, unfiltered, done differently, against, break, challenge, refuse
- Profanity (when appropriate to brand voice) is authentic here
- Humor is sharp, dark, or sardonic — not gentle

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- "We/they lied to you about [topic]" exposé content
- Hot takes that name what's wrong with the industry
- Destruction of the old way + reveal of the better way
- Anti-aspirational content (mocking luxury brand tropes)
- Radical transparency / brand rants
- The "us vs. them" narrative (done carefully)
- Manifestos and public declarations

**Social Voice:**
- Short, punchy, provocative captions
- Raw photography — grainy, high contrast, or deliberately rough
- Controversy is content (within brand values)
- Comment section engagement is combative-friendly
- Avoid: corporate polish, excessive hashtags, playing it safe

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Black, blood red, electric yellow, raw orange, graffiti color pops
- **Avoid:** Pastels, corporate blue, anything that reads as "safe"
- **Typography:** Distressed typefaces, stencil fonts, bold condensed, or deliberately rough
- **Imagery:** Gritty, urban, countercultural, raw authenticity, flawed beauty
- **Texture:** Concrete, spray paint, torn edges, industrial metal, leather

**Font Pairings:**
- Bebas Neue (heading) + Barlow Condensed (body) + Space Mono (accent)
- Black Han Sans (heading) + DM Sans (body) + IBM Plex Mono (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Black (#0A0A0A)
- Accent: Blood red (#CC0000) or electric yellow (#FFD100)
- Secondary: Raw white (#F0EDE4) or concrete grey
- Avoid gradients — flat, raw color only

### 05. THE EXPLORER

**Core desire:** Discovery, freedom, adventure, experiencing the new  
**Core fear:** Being trapped, conformity, emptiness, missing out  
**Brand promise:** "We help you discover more — of the world, of yourself."  
**Superpower:** Curiosity — this brand is always moving forward  
**Shadow side:** Rootlessness, commitment-phobia, inability to arrive

**Brand Examples:** Patagonia, The North Face, Jeep, National Geographic, Lululemon, REI, GoPro

**Voice + Tone:**
- Adventurous, curious, free-spirited, authentic
- Invites the audience into a journey, not a destination
- Uses words like: discover, explore, venture, seek, roam, beyond, wild, journey, authentic
- Aspirational without being aspirationally wealthy — earned freedom, not bought
- Conversational + passionate

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- "We went to [place] so you could see this" journey content
- Behind-the-scenes of the process/adventure
- Discovery-framed education ("What we found when we...")
- "The road less traveled" positioning
- User-generated adventure content
- Philosophical "what does [concept] mean to you?" posts
- Bucket list / goal-setting content

**Social Voice:**
- Longer captions that tell a story
- Golden hour, vast landscapes, lone figure in nature photography
- Reels of adventure and movement
- Strong travel/outdoor community engagement
- Avoid: corporate rigidity, excess polish, indoor studio shots

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Earth tones, deep forest green, burnt orange, stone grey, sky blue, deep navy
- **Avoid:** Hyper-saturation, neon, anything synthetic-feeling
- **Typography:** Sturdy serifs or clean adventure-ready sans-serifs. Nothing precious.
- **Imagery:** Vast landscapes, human in nature, movement, golden light, authentic gear
- **Texture:** Canvas, worn leather, topographic lines, natural materials

**Font Pairings:**
- Libre Baskerville (heading) + Source Sans Pro (body) + Courier Prime (accent)
- Playfair Display (heading) + Nunito Sans (body) + Space Mono (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Deep forest (#1E3A2F) or slate navy (#1A2744)
- Secondary: Burnt sienna (#A0522D) or stone (#8B7355)
- Accent: Sky (#4A90D9) or sun gold (#D4A017)
- Background: Warm off-white or aged parchment

### 06. THE CREATOR

**Core desire:** Creation, vision, artistic expression, building something with meaning  
**Core fear:** Having a mediocre vision, creating something inauthentic, being ordinary  
**Brand promise:** "We help you build something that didn't exist before."  
**Superpower:** Imagination — this brand sees what isn't there yet  
**Shadow side:** Perfectionism, inaccessibility, self-indulgence

**Brand Examples:** Apple, Adobe, Lego, Pinterest, Etsy, Squarespace, Moleskine, Canva

**Voice + Tone:**
- Visionary, thoughtful, craft-obsessed, original
- Talks about *why* something was made, not just what it is
- Uses words like: craft, create, build, design, imagine, vision, original, beautiful, intentional, make
- Celebrates the process as much as the result
- Intellectual but not academic

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Process content ("Here's how we built this")
- "Why we made this decision" design rationale
- Work-in-progress and behind-the-scenes creation
- Finished work showcase with story behind it
- Inspiration and reference content ("What's been inspiring us")
- Collaboration spotlights
- "What does good design/craft look like?" thought leadership

**Social Voice:**
- Considered captions — process, intent, and meaning
- High craft photography or deliberately artistic visuals
- Carousels showing process → result
- Aesthetic feeds with intentional visual rhythm
- Avoid: Cheap stock photos, sloppy visuals, rushed content

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Varies by sub-category but always: intentional, deliberate, nothing accidental
  - Tech-adjacent Creator: Near black, minimal white, accent color
  - Artisan Creator: Warm earth tones, natural textures, handmade feel
  - Digital Creator: Gradient-forward, vibrant accent on neutral
- **Typography:** High personality fonts that reflect the brand's creative direction
- **Imagery:** The work itself. Process shots. Objects with intention and beauty.
- **Texture:** Depends on sub-brand — digital (none), artisan (linen, wood, paper)

**Font Pairings:**
- Cormorant Garamond (heading) + Lora (body) + DM Mono (accent) — artisan
- Space Grotesk (heading) + DM Sans (body) + IBM Plex Mono (accent) — digital
- Abril Fatface (heading) + Merriweather (body) + Source Code Pro (accent) — editorial

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Artisan Creator: Warm cream (#F5EDD6), terracotta (#C4603B), charcoal (#2D2926)
- Digital Creator: Near-black (#141414), white, electric accent (purple #7B2FBE or teal #00B4D8)
- Editorial Creator: Deep navy (#0E1B4D), gold (#D4AF37), parchment (#F2EAD3)

### 07. THE RULER

**Core desire:** Control, order, authority, leading from the top  
**Core fear:** Chaos, being overthrown, losing control or status  
**Brand promise:** "We are the best — and we help you be the best too."  
**Superpower:** Leadership — this brand sets the standard others follow  
**Shadow side:** Rigidity, elitism, inability to admit mistakes

**Brand Examples:** Rolex, Mercedes-Benz, American Express (Black), McKinsey, Harvard, Microsoft, Louis Vuitton

**Voice + Tone:**
- Authoritative, precise, measured, confident (never brash)
- Speaks with the assumption of expertise — never needs to prove itself
- Uses words like: excellence, authority, precision, leadership, standard, benchmark, proven, legacy, command
- Formal but not stiff — dignified
- Never apologetic. Never explains itself. Never discounts.

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Industry thought leadership (set the agenda, don't follow it)
- Standards and benchmarks ("What separates good from excellence")
- Case studies with outcomes — authority through results
- Exclusive access content (behind closed doors)
- Awards, rankings, recognition — social proof of dominance
- "The definitive guide to [topic]" — own the frame
- Executive / founder perspective on industry direction

**Social Voice:**
- Short, authoritative statements — not questions
- Elevated photography: architecture, boardrooms, fine materials
- Minimal hashtags — leaders don't need to be found, they're sought
- Engage selectively — rulers don't chase, they attract
- Avoid: humor that undercuts authority, discount messaging, follower-chasing tactics

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Deep navy, dark green, black, gold, burgundy, platinum — always rich, never cheap
- **Avoid:** Bright primary colors, casual fonts, anything that reads as accessible/mass market
- **Typography:** Elegant serifs with weight. Think Cinzel, Cormorant, Garamond. Nothing round or casual.
- **Imagery:** Architecture, precision objects, power materials (marble, leather, gold), distance and composure
- **Texture:** Marble, embossed leather, engraved metal, linen — premium tactility

**Font Pairings:**
- Cinzel (heading) + Cormorant Garamond (body) + Montserrat (accent) — institutional
- EB Garamond (heading) + Libre Baskerville (body) + Raleway (accent) — boutique authority
- Playfair Display (heading) + Crimson Pro (body) + Space Mono (accent) — editorial authority

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Deep navy (#0D1B2A), dark forest (#182F23), or onyx (#1A1A1A)
- Secondary: Burgundy (#6B1E2E) or deep teal (#0E3D40)
- Accent: Gold (#C9A84C), platinum (#C0C0C0), or ivory (#F5F0E0)
- Background: Parchment (#F2EAD3) or deep field (primary color)

### 08. THE MAGICIAN

**Core desire:** Transformation, making dreams real, understanding fundamental laws  
**Core fear:** Negative consequences, unintended harm, being exposed as a charlatan  
**Brand promise:** "We will transform your reality."  
**Superpower:** Vision — this brand sees possibility where others see limitation  
**Shadow side:** Manipulation, promising too much, losing touch with reality

**Brand Examples:** Disney, Apple (product launch era), TED, Dyson, Tesla, Peloton, Headspace

**Voice + Tone:**
- Visionary, transformative, awe-inspiring, mystical yet grounded
- Speaks in outcomes and transformation — not features
- Uses words like: transform, vision, possible, imagine, change, illuminate, reveal, shift, unlock, magic
- Creates anticipation and wonder
- Confident in the promise of transformation — not vague about it

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Transformation stories (before → after with depth)
- "What if [thing everyone assumes] didn't have to be that way?"
- Behind-the-transformation content
- Revelatory insight posts ("The real reason why [thing] doesn't work")
- Vision and future-state content
- Experiential content — content that creates a feeling
- "What we believe is possible" brand belief posts

**Social Voice:**
- Cinematic, evocative captions
- Video performs extremely well — atmosphere and journey
- Before/after transformation content
- Create intrigue — not everything is explained immediately
- Avoid: Overpromising without delivery, feature lists, mundane how-to content

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Deep jewel tones, midnight blue, violet, gold, electric teal — a sense of depth and mystery
- **Avoid:** Flat, literal, ordinary-feeling palettes
- **Typography:** Elegant display fonts with personality — something that feels like a spell
- **Imagery:** Light from darkness, transformation, before/after, elements (fire, water, light)
- **Texture:** Velvet, glass, light refraction, gradients that feel dimensional

**Font Pairings:**
- Cinzel Decorative (heading) + Cormorant Garamond (body) + EB Garamond (accent)
- Playfair Display (heading) + Lora (body) + Raleway (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Midnight blue (#0D1B4B) or deep violet (#2D0D5E)
- Secondary: Teal (#005F73) or forest (#1B4332)
- Accent: Gold (#D4AF37) or electric teal (#00B4D8)
- Background: Near-black with subtle warm undertone, or deep jewel tone

### 09. THE LOVER

**Core desire:** Intimacy, passion, sensory pleasure, connection, being desired  
**Core fear:** Being alone, unwanted, unloved, ordinary  
**Brand promise:** "We make you feel desired and connected."  
**Superpower:** Passion — this brand makes everything feel more alive  
**Shadow side:** Promiscuity, superficiality, vanity, seduction without substance

**Brand Examples:** Chanel, Victoria's Secret, Godiva, Häagen-Dazs, Jo Malone, Cartier, Lush

**Voice + Tone:**
- Sensual, intimate, warm, passionate, sophisticated
- Speaks in sensation — what things feel like, smell like, taste like
- Uses words like: desire, passion, intimate, sensory, indulge, cherish, adore, exquisite, devoted
- Second-person intimate: "You deserve..." "Imagine..."
- Whispers, doesn't shout

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Sensory descriptions (taste, touch, smell, texture)
- "For the one who..." audience identification
- Romantic occasion content (Valentine's, anniversaries, self-care)
- Gifting content with emotional context
- Behind-the-craft of something beautiful
- The feeling after (not the product itself)
- "This is what you deserve" self-love framing

**Social Voice:**
- Rich, evocative captions — short but atmospheric
- Deep-color, intimate photography
- Food, fragrance, skin, fabric — close-up sensory shots
- Love languages as content pillars
- Avoid: clinical language, pricing front-and-center, harsh lighting

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Deep rose, burgundy, champagne, blush, ivory, dark chocolate, gold
- **Avoid:** Bright primaries, harsh contrast, anything cold or clinical
- **Typography:** Romantic serifs, elegant scripts, nothing geometric or hard
- **Imagery:** Intimate moments, beautiful objects close-up, skin and texture, candlelight and warmth
- **Texture:** Velvet, silk, petals, cream — soft, sensory, luxurious

**Font Pairings:**
- Cormorant Garamond (heading) + Crimson Pro (body) + Playfair Display (accent)
- EB Garamond (heading) + Libre Baskerville (body) + Raleway Italic (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Deep rose (#8B1A4A) or dark plum (#4A1040)
- Secondary: Champagne (#F7E7CE) or blush (#F2C4C4)
- Accent: Gold (#C9A84C) or rose gold (#B76E79)
- Background: Warm cream (#FDF6EC) or deep burgundy

### 10. THE CAREGIVER

**Core desire:** Service, protection, nurturing others, giving without expectation  
**Core fear:** Selfishness, ingratitude, being unable to help  
**Brand promise:** "We take care of you — and those you love."  
**Superpower:** Empathy — this brand genuinely cares and it shows  
**Shadow side:** Martyrdom, enabling dependence, smothering

**Brand Examples:** Johnson & Johnson, TOMS, Whole Foods, Mayo Clinic, UNICEF, Pampers, Burt's Bees

**Voice + Tone:**
- Warm, nurturing, empathetic, reassuring, sincere
- Always centered on the other person's wellbeing — not the brand
- Uses words like: care, support, nourish, protect, help, safe, together, family, gentle, trust
- Acknowledges difficulty before offering solution
- Never lectures. Validates first.

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Educational content that genuinely helps (not a lead-in to a pitch)
- "Here's how to take care of yourself / someone you love" practical content
- Community and belonging content
- Social impact and giving-back content
- Real testimonials with emotional depth
- "Signs you might need support" awareness content
- Seasonal care reminders (winter wellness, back-to-school prep)

**Social Voice:**
- Warm, longer captions — no rush, no pressure
- Real people photography — diverse, including vulnerable moments
- Parenting, aging, illness, mental health handled with dignity
- High engagement with comments — always responds with care
- Avoid: Fearmongering, guilt-tripping, clinical distance

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Warm sage, soft teal, warm white, lavender, peach, light honey gold
- **Avoid:** Cold blues, harsh contrast, aggressive black
- **Typography:** Rounded, warm fonts. Nothing sharp or authoritative.
- **Imagery:** Human connection — hands, hugs, families, nature, warm light
- **Texture:** Knit, soft cotton, organic materials, natural warmth

**Font Pairings:**
- Merriweather (heading) + Nunito (body) + Lato (accent)
- Playfair Display (heading) + Source Sans Pro (body) + Raleway (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Warm sage (#7A9E7E) or soft teal (#4D9E9E)
- Secondary: Peach (#FFBE98) or lavender (#C4AED0)
- Accent: Honey gold (#E8A838) or warm coral
- Background: Warm white (#FAFAF6) or soft cream

### 11. THE JESTER

**Core desire:** Joy, laughter, living in the moment, making people laugh  
**Core fear:** Boredom, being dull, taking things too seriously, being found unfunny  
**Brand promise:** "We make life more fun — and we're not sorry about it."  
**Superpower:** Joy — this brand makes people feel lighter just by existing  
**Shadow side:** Superficiality, offensiveness, not being taken seriously when it matters

**Brand Examples:** Old Spice, Dollar Shave Club, Wendy's, M&Ms, Ben & Jerry's, MailChimp, Cards Against Humanity

**Voice + Tone:**
- Playful, witty, self-aware, irreverent, quick
- Never takes itself seriously — uses humor as the primary tool
- Uses words like: fun, hilarious, ridiculous, come on, obviously, honestly, just saying
- Pop culture references, wordplay, absurdism
- Knows the line between funny and offensive — stays funny

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Meme-based content native to the platform
- Brand personality content ("If we were a person, we'd be...")
- Reactive / trending audio content
- Self-deprecating brand humor
- Unexpected product use cases (absurdist)
- Hot take + comedic twist
- "We can't believe we have to say this but..." content

**Social Voice:**
- Short, punchy, often single-sentence captions
- Conversational reply to comments — the brand has personality
- Trending audio and formats embraced immediately
- Extremely high engagement — the brand is the entertainment
- Avoid: Stiff corporate speak, playing it safe, being late to trends

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Bright, saturated, unexpected combinations — nothing muted
- **Avoid:** Desaturation, minimalism, overly serious layouts
- **Typography:** Expressive, playful fonts — can be slightly imperfect
- **Imagery:** Exaggerated expressions, bright backgrounds, unexpected juxtapositions
- **Texture:** Bold, graphic, high contrast — print-inspired

**Font Pairings:**

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- No rules — but always: bright, intentional, high energy

- Example: Lime (#B5E853) + electric blue (#004FFF) + tangerine (#FF6B35)

### 12. THE SAGE

**Core desire:** Truth, wisdom, knowledge, understanding  
**Core fear:** Deception, ignorance, being seen as uninformed  
**Brand promise:** "We help you find the truth and understand the world."  
**Superpower:** Clarity — this brand cuts through noise and reveals what's real  
**Shadow side:** Detachment, paralysis by analysis, arrogance of expertise

**Brand Examples:** Google, BBC, The Economist, McKinsey, TED, MIT, Quora, Wikipedia, Edelman

**Voice + Tone:**
- Authoritative, clear, informed, balanced, precise
- Positions information as the product — the value is the knowledge
- Uses words like: research, evidence, understanding, insight, data, truth, expert, informed, strategic
- Confident in data and source — cites, attributes, shows work
- Never sensationalizes. Respects the reader's intelligence.

**Content Strategy Hooks:**
- Data-driven insight posts ("The research shows...")
- "What most people get wrong about [topic]"
- Expert interviews and credentialing
- Long-form explainers — carousels, threads, newsletters
- "The full breakdown of [complex topic]" content
- Counter-intuitive insights backed by evidence
- Industry analysis and trend forecasting

**Social Voice:**
- Longer captions with substance — the value is the content
- Infographics and data visualization
- Thread-style content on LinkedIn/X
- Quote authority figures and data sources
- Avoid: Fluff, unsupported claims, hype, urgent/emotional hooks

**Visual Direction:**
- **Colors:** Deep navy, forest green, grey, white, midnight blue — serious, credible palettes
- **Avoid:** Saturated bright colors, casual fonts, playful layouts
- **Typography:** Clean, readable, credible. Strong serifs or clean sans-serifs.
- **Imagery:** Data visualizations, architecture, books, expert settings, editorial photography
- **Texture:** Paper, linen, minimal — let the content breathe

**Font Pairings:**
- EB Garamond (heading) + Source Serif Pro (body) + IBM Plex Mono (accent)
- Libre Baskerville (heading) + Open Sans (body) + Roboto Mono (accent)

**Color Palette Anchors:**
- Primary: Deep navy (#0D2137) or slate (#2D3A4A)
- Secondary: Forest (#1E3A2F) or burgundy (#4A1020)
- Accent: Warm gold (#D4A017) or teal (#00858A)
- Background: Light grey (#F4F4F4) or warm white

## The Archetype Wheel — Quadrant System

The 12 archetypes map to four motivational quadrants arranged in opposing pairs. Use this to understand *why* two archetypes are compatible or incompatible, and to navigate quickly when a brand sits on the boundary between two.

```
              ORDER / STABILITY
                    |
          Innocent—Sage—Ruler
                    |
SOCIAL ——Caregiver      Creator—— EGO / MASTERY
       ——Everyman        Hero——
       ——Lover          Explorer——
                    |
          Jester—Outlaw—(Rebel)
                    |
              FREEDOM / DISRUPTION
```

### The Four Quadrants

| Quadrant | Core Motivation | Archetypes (center → edge) |
|----------|----------------|---------------------------|
| **Order** | Stability, structure, safety | Ruler (center) → Sage → Innocent |
| **Ego / Mastery** | Achievement, growth, self-expression | Hero (center) → Creator → Explorer |
| **Freedom** | Change, disruption, adventure | Outlaw (center) → Explorer → Jester |
| **Social / Belonging** | Connection, community, care | Caregiver → Everyman → Lover |

**Key insight:** Archetypes adjacent on the wheel are most compatible. Archetypes opposite each other (Order vs. Freedom, Ego vs. Social) create the highest-conflict pairings. This is why Ruler + Jester or Innocent + Outlaw fail — they sit in directly opposing quadrants.

### Quadrant-Based Pairing Logic

Instead of memorizing pairing rules, use the wheel:
- **Same quadrant** = very compatible (same motivational core)
- **Adjacent quadrant** = compatible (share one driver)
- **Opposite quadrant** = incompatible (motivational conflict)

## Primary:Secondary Expression Ratio

**Rule: Primary archetype = 60-70% of all brand expression. Secondary = 20-25%. Remaining 5-15% = humanity/texture.**

This ratio governs:
- Caption writing (every 3 posts should sound clearly like the primary, 1 may lean secondary)
- Visual production (primary archetype's visual language dominates; secondary adds dimension)
- Campaign planning (primary archetype owns the brand's signature campaign; secondary shows up in supporting content)

**If the ratio flips or becomes 50/50, the brand loses identity.** A Ruler brand that posts too much Caregiver-style warmth starts to read as confused. A Creator brand that borrows too much Hero achievement-language starts to feel like it's overcompensating.

## Authenticity Calibration by Archetype (2024-2025 Shift)

Research from 2024-2025 shows younger audiences (Gen Z, younger Millennials) have shifted from aspirational self-identification to **authentic self-identification** — they connect with brands that reflect who they *actually are*, not who they want to be.

This changes how several archetypes should be voiced:

| Archetype | Old Approach | 2025-Authentic Approach |
|-----------|-------------|------------------------|
| Innocent | "Life is perfect and beautiful" | "We believe simple is better — and that's enough" |
| Hero | "You can be extraordinary" | "You're already putting in the work — we see that" |
| Everyman | "We're all in this together" | "We're one of you — not a brand pretending to be" |
| Explorer | "Live your best adventure life" | "The everyday can be an adventure — it already is" |
| Caregiver | "We take care of everyone" | "You're not alone in this — we've been here too" |

**Archetypes least affected by this shift:** Ruler, Sage, Magician (their authority and transformation promises remain aspirational by design).

## Agentic AI Consistency Note (2025)

As brands increasingly use AI for content creation, archetype consistency becomes a governance challenge — AI tools default to a generic, helpful, neutral voice that belongs to no archetype. 

**Rule: Every AI-generated content piece must be passed through the archetype filter before publishing.**

Archetype filter test:
1. Read the copy out loud. Does it sound like the brand's character or like a helpful AI assistant?
2. Does it use words from the archetype's vocabulary (see Voice + Tone sections above)?
3. Would a competitor with a different archetype plausibly post this exact content? (If yes, it's not archetypal enough.)
