# Legal Deliverables Checklist — for Attorney Call

**Purpose:** A focused brief for the attorney call so the conversation is efficient and the deliverables are clear. Goal: get the legal foundation in place to sell Brand Blueprint to first paying customers within 7-10 days.

**Estimated attorney engagement:** 90-minute initial call + ~5-10 hours drafting = $1,500-$3,500 typical for a startup attorney. Higher if you choose a firm; lower if you choose a solo practitioner.

**Recommended attorney type:** Startup / tech transactions attorney with SaaS + IP experience. Avoid general practice. Look for someone who has worked with creator-economy or solo founders.

---

## Context to share with attorney (before the call)

Send this in an intake email so they're prepped:

> I'm launching a digital product called **Brand Blueprint** under my company **IEI Ventures**. It's an AI-assisted brand-development service rooted in my proprietary methodology, **The IEI Brand System**. Customers pay $750-$3,000 to receive a brand foundation (messaging, logo, website, GTM checklist) generated by AI and reviewed by me personally. The methodology is proprietary IP I developed over years of facilitator work. I need to launch within 2 weeks. Please advise on the deliverables list attached.

Attach: this checklist + `PRODUCT_FRAMING.md` + `STAGE1_LAUNCH_PLAN.md`.

## Deliverables I need from the attorney

### 1. Terms of Service (CRITICAL — blocks launch)
A click-through TOS that the customer must accept at Stripe checkout. Must address:
- [ ] Customer owns the OUTPUTS (brand messaging, logo, website copy) we deliver to them
- [ ] IEI Ventures owns the SYSTEM (IEI Brand System methodology, software, prompts, frameworks)
- [ ] Customer grants IEI Ventures a limited license to use their submitted info (name, business, story) to fulfill the service
- [ ] No warranty on commercial success of the brand
- [ ] Limitation of liability (capped at amount paid by customer)
- [ ] Refund policy: full refund within 24 hours of purchase IF intake call has not begun; no refund after intake begins (because work has commenced)
- [ ] Trademark collision disclaimer (customer responsible for clearing their generated brand name + logo with their own trademark counsel before commercial use)
- [ ] AI-generated content disclosure (per FTC + state AI disclosure trends in 2025-2026)
- [ ] Governing law + venue
- [ ] Modification clause (can update TOS with notice)

### 2. Privacy Policy (CRITICAL — blocks launch)
Required by Stripe, by California (CCPA/CPRA), and by Apple/Google if there's ever a mobile app. Must address:
- [ ] What data we collect (name, email, payment via Stripe, business info submitted during intake, chat transcripts)
- [ ] How we store it (cloud provider, encryption at rest and in transit)
- [ ] Who we share it with (Stripe, Anthropic for AI generation, email provider, no other third parties)
- [ ] Retention policy (how long we keep data after delivery — recommend 90 days post-delivery for support, then deletion or anonymization)
- [ ] User rights (CCPA delete, access, opt-out of sale — we don't sell data so this is easy)
- [ ] GDPR if accepting EU customers (recommend NOT for MVP — geofence at checkout)
- [ ] Cookie policy (minimal — just session cookies + Stripe + analytics if used)
- [ ] Contact for privacy requests

### 3. IP / Confidentiality Click-Through Agreement (CRITICAL — blocks launch)
A separate checkbox at Stripe checkout, distinct from TOS. This mirrors the live workshop's IP agreement (Step 2 of facilitator guide). Must address:
- [ ] Customer's submitted business info, ideas, stories are THEIR intellectual property
- [ ] IEI Ventures will not share customer's submitted info publicly or with other customers without explicit written permission
- [ ] Customer may share their OWN outputs publicly
- [ ] The IEI Brand System methodology, workshop scripts, AI prompts, and software remain IEI Ventures property
- [ ] Customer agrees not to reverse-engineer, copy, or resell the IEI Brand System framework
- [ ] If recorded (intake call, etc.), the recording belongs to IEI Ventures and won't be shared publicly without customer permission

### 4. Entity Structure Advice (IMPORTANT — affects future licensing)
Per the GTM plan: *"Confirm with an attorney whether each product should be its own LLC under IEI Ventures or a product line within it. This affects liability, licensing, and any future sale of a single product."*
- [ ] Should Brand Blueprint be its own LLC under IEI Ventures, or a product line within IEI Ventures LLC?
- [ ] If IEI Ventures isn't formed yet — should it be Delaware LLC, home-state LLC, or C-Corp? (Trade-offs for fundraising + tax)
- [ ] IP assignment: are Tab + Ashley individually assignors to IEI Ventures? Need written IP assignment agreements (the GTM plan flags this explicitly).
- [ ] Operating agreement between Tab + Ashley if both are owners

### 5. Trademark Filing Strategy (IMPORTANT — clock starts on filing date)
Per the GTM plan: *"Register the product names and logos. Inexpensive, fast, and the core asset for a software product."*
- [ ] File **"Brand Blueprint"** word mark (Class 42 — SaaS / software services; possibly Class 35 — business consulting)
- [ ] File **"IEI Ventures"** word mark (Class 35 + Class 42)
- [ ] Optional: file **"Ideas Equal Income"** as a slogan/tagline mark
- [ ] Optional: file **"The IEI Brand System"** as a methodology/services mark
- [ ] Discuss: which proprietary phrases are worth ®? ("Lock It In," "From Idea to Income," "The Fourfold Path to Meaningful Marketing")
- [ ] DIY via USPTO TEAS Plus ($250-$350/mark/class) vs. paralegal-filed (~$500-$700 total) vs. attorney-filed (~$1,500/mark) — recommend DIY or paralegal for MVP
- [ ] Backup names ready in case primary marks get opposed

### 6. AI Training / Output Disclaimers (IMPORTANT — emerging legal area)
The legal landscape on AI-generated content is shifting fast (2025-2026 saw several state laws + FTC guidance).
- [ ] Disclosure that outputs are AI-generated with human review (some states require this)
- [ ] Disclaimer that AI may produce errors and final responsibility lies with customer
- [ ] Customer warrants that submitted content doesn't infringe third-party rights (their business name, their story, etc.)
- [ ] We don't train Anthropic's models on customer data (Anthropic confirms this in their commercial terms — worth referencing)

### 7. Future-Stage Templates (NOT BLOCKING launch but worth drafting now)
Ask the attorney to draft as a follow-up engagement so we're ready for Stage 2-4:
- [ ] Customer NDA template (for cohort customers — used when a customer wants to share IP with the group)
- [ ] White-label / licensing agreement template (for Stage 4 of GTM plan — music app and brand app)
- [ ] Independent contractor template (for any freelancers you bring on for Stage 2 scale)
- [ ] Master Services Agreement template (for enterprise customers in Stage 3)

## Questions to ask the attorney during the call

1. What's your typical turnaround for TOS + Privacy + IP clickthrough drafts? (Need within 5-7 days to launch.)
2. Flat fee or hourly for this initial package?
3. Do you have a startup retainer option for ongoing questions?
4. Any state-specific issues if I'm in [your state] and selling nationwide?
5. Recommendation on entity structure given the GTM plan (single LLC vs. holding company + subs)?
6. Any red flags in the Brand Blueprint offer as described?
7. Should the IP clickthrough be a separate document or part of TOS?
8. Anything specific to AI-generated content I should know in 2026?

## Red flags to watch for in attorney selection

- Charges to "review" your business idea (not what you need; you need contracts)
- No SaaS / digital product experience
- No examples of past work with creator-economy clients
- Quotes a flat fee >$5,000 for the launch package without explaining scope
- Reluctant to put estimated timeline in writing
