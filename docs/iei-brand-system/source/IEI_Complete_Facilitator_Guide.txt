PART 2: LESSON — The One Brilliant Idea Framework

TIMING: 10 minutes

PURPOSE: Teach the concept simply and clearly before participants do the activity. They should be able to explain the Brilliant Idea framework to someone else before they try to apply it.



[ TAB SAYS ]

Okay. So here's the framework.

It's two lists and one equation.



List one: Things I Love.

What do you enjoy doing? What are your passions? What would you do even if nobody paid you?

What topics could you talk about for hours?



List two: Things I'm Good At.

What skills do you have? What do people come to you for?

What have you been paid to do — or could be paid to do?

What do you do better than most people around you?



And then — the equation.

You look at both lists. And you find ONE thing from each column that could work together.

Something that makes you say: oh. THAT. That's it.



Now — I want to give you a silly example so you can see how this works.

Let's say I love cats. And I love coffee. I'm also good at creating cozy spaces.

What do I do with that? Cat cafe. That's a business.

It sounds ridiculous. But someone did that. And it worked.

Because they took what they loved, married it with what they were good at, and made something real.



Now here's what I want you to understand.

This is for people who have ALREADY been doing something.

You've been running a business. You've been freelancing. You've had a side hustle.

You're not starting from zero — you're starting from experience.

The goal of this exercise is not to invent something new.

The goal is to look at what's already in you — and get clear on the ONE thing to build from.



One thing. Not five. Not a menu. One.

That's your brilliant idea. And that's where we start.



[ FACILITATOR NOTE ]

The cat cafe example usually gets a laugh — use it. It loosens the room and makes the concept feel approachable.

Key line to watch for: 'You're not starting from zero — you're starting from experience.' This is important for this audience. They may feel like they don't have enough or haven't done enough. Remind them that what they've already been doing IS the material.

Before moving to the activity, check in: 'Does this make sense so far? Any questions before we go into the exercise?' Keep it quick — 60 seconds max.

PART 2: LESSON — The ABCs of Branding

TIMING: 15 minutes

PURPOSE: Introduce Authentic Brand Creation through the 'brand is like a baby' concept. Teach the 5 defining questions in plain human language — not corporate speak. This is where participants begin to see their own brand more clearly.



[ TAB SAYS ]

Here's what I want you to understand about your brand.

Your brand is like a baby.



When a baby is born — that baby already has a personality.

A vibe. A whole presence.

But it can't speak for itself yet.

Someone has to learn it. Understand it. Give it language.

And the better you understand that baby — the better you can communicate who they are to the world.



Your brand is the same way.

It already exists inside you.

It has a personality. A voice. A way of showing up.

But most of us have never sat down to actually LEARN our own brand.

We haven't given it language.

And so when someone asks — what do you do? — we fumble.

Not because we don't know. But because we've never defined it clearly enough to say it out loud.



What I teach is called the ABCs of Branding.

Authentic Brand Creation.

And I need YOU to embody your brand.

Not perform it. Not polish it. Embody it.

Because that's what authentic means.

It comes from YOU. From your story. From your life. From what you know.



So we're going to answer five questions.

And I'm going to ask them in a different way than you've probably heard before.

Because most brand frameworks ask them in corporate speak.

I want to ask them in human speak.



THE 5 DEFINING QUESTIONS — Taught One at a Time



[ QUESTION 1: WHO ARE YOU? ]

Not your job title. Not your LinkedIn headline. Not your resume.

I mean — if you walked into a room, how would somebody describe you?

What's the energy you bring? What do people feel when they're around you?

What is the thing you've lived through — the experience, the journey, the story — that positions you to do this work?

Because your brand isn't separate from your story. Your brand IS your story, packaged as a solution.



Coaching line if someone is stuck: 'What do people always say about you? What's the compliment you get over and over?'



[ QUESTION 2: WHAT DO YOU ACTUALLY DO? ]

Not the fancy version. Not the polished pitch.

What activities do you do — or have you done — involving your brilliant idea?

What would someone watch you do if they shadowed you for a day?

We're not writing the one-liner yet. We're just naming the activities. Plain language.

List them out. Everything. We'll distill it down in a minute.



FACILITATOR NOTE: This question strips away the marketing language and gets to the real work. People often realize they do more than they thought — or they realize they've been describing their outputs instead of their process.

Coaching line: 'Don't describe the result yet. Just tell me what you actually DO. The verbs.'



[ QUESTION 3: WHO DO YOU DO IT FOR? ]

Who have you already done this for?

Who is the specific person you want to serve?

And I need you to get specific. Not 'entrepreneurs.' Not 'small businesses.'

What KIND of person? What stage are they in? What are they dealing with right now?

What is the thing keeping them up at night that your brilliant idea can solve?



FACILITATOR NOTE: This is where most people resist. They want to say 'everyone.' Push back gently every time. 'That's still too broad. Who specifically? If you could fill your client roster with one type of person — who would it be?'



[ QUESTION 4: WHAT MAKES YOU STAND OUT? ]

There are other people in your space. Other people with a similar brilliant idea.

Why would someone choose YOU?

This is usually three things: your story, your method, and your results.

What have you lived through that gives you credibility?

What's the specific way you do what you do?

And what happens after someone works with you? What changes for them?



FACILITATOR NOTE: People often underestimate this one. They think they don't stand out. Remind them: 'Your story alone makes you different. Nobody has lived what you've lived and built what you're building from it. That IS your differentiation.'



[ QUESTION 5: HOW DO YOU DO IT? ]

Your process. Your method. Your secret sauce.

How do you get someone from where they are to where they want to be?

Is it a system? A series of sessions? A framework? A product?

Describe it simply. How would you explain it to a friend?

Because here's what I learned — oftentimes, your HOW is exactly what makes you special.

My how is a system. A series of frameworks and questions.

And that system IS IEI. The how became the brand.



FACILITATOR NOTE: End the lesson here and connect back to the D.U.M.I example: 'These are the exact questions I should have asked Jaydee before I built that website. Now you have them. Let's apply them to YOUR brand.'

PART 2: LESSON — What Goes Into an Offering

TIMING: 15 minutes

PURPOSE: Teach the 6-part offering framework simply and clearly. Every participant should be able to name all 6 parts before they start the activity. This is not about having a perfect offer — it is about having a defined one.



[ TAB SAYS ]

Here's what most people do when they try to create an offering.

They name it something cool. They pick a price. And they stop there.

And then they wonder why nobody buys it.



Because naming and pricing is not an offering.

An offering is a complete picture.

It answers six questions. And ALL six need to be answered.

If even one is missing — your offer is leaking.



Here are the six:



ONE — WHO is it for?

Not everyone. The specific person from Module 3.

Your offer should speak directly to that one person.

If someone reads your offer description and says 'that's me' — you've got it right.



TWO — What PROBLEM does it solve?

The specific pain. The thing keeping your ideal client up at night.

Not the general frustration — the exact problem.

If you can name their problem better than they can — they will trust you instantly.



THREE — What IS it?

Define it clearly. Is it a session? A package? A workshop? A product?

How long does it take? How is it delivered?

One-on-one or group? Virtual or in person?

Clarity here is what makes someone feel safe saying yes.



FOUR — What does it take to CREATE it?

This one is for you, not the client.

What time, tools, materials, or preparation do you need?

This is how you know if the price makes sense.

If it takes you 10 hours to deliver and you charge $97 — we have a problem.

Know your inputs before you set your price.



FIVE — What are the DELIVERABLES?

What does the client actually walk away with?

List everything. The session, the document, the recording, the follow-up.

The more concrete the deliverables — the easier it is to sell.

'You will walk away with X, Y, and Z' is far more powerful than 'you will feel better.'



SIX — What is the OUTCOME?
