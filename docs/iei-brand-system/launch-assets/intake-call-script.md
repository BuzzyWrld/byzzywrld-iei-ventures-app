# Brand Blueprint — Intake Call Script (60 Minutes)

**Purpose:** Tab's operational playbook for every Stage 1 customer intake call. Structured around the 6 modules of The IEI Brand System, with timing, transition language, and what to capture for the AI generation step.

**Format:** 60-minute Zoom call, recorded (with customer consent).
**Output:** Filled intake form (pasted into existing `/new/deep` flow) + call notes + any uploaded assets (existing logo, photos, inspiration links).

---

## PRE-CALL SETUP (5 min before)

- [ ] Pull up customer's purchase confirmation + which tier they bought
- [ ] Have the intake form open in a second window (existing `/new/deep` page)
- [ ] Recording set up + ready
- [ ] Glass of water, no phone, do-not-disturb on
- [ ] Pull up the customer's website or socials if they shared in their booking (quick 2-min scan)

---

## MIN 0–5 · OPEN + IP AGREEMENT REMINDER

**TAB SAYS (verbatim suggestion):**

> Hey [NAME] — welcome. I'm Tab. Thank you for trusting me with this.
>
> Quick housekeeping. I'm going to record this — that was in the IP agreement you signed at checkout, but I want to confirm verbally too. Cool with you? Great.
>
> What happens in this call stays between you and me. Your ideas are your intellectual property. The method I'm using — The IEI Brand System — that stays mine. Anything we build for you in the next five days is yours forever. Sound good?
>
> One more thing — there are no wrong answers today. If I ask something and you don't know, just say "I don't know." That's useful information. We'll work with what's there.
>
> Ready? Let's go.

**CAPTURE:** verbal consent to recording (Y/N), any concerns raised.

---

## MIN 5–12 · MODULE 1 — ONE BRILLIANT IDEA

**TAB SAYS:**

> Before we get into branding — I want to start with the idea itself.
>
> Tell me — what's the ONE thing at the intersection of what you love and what you're good at? Some people call it a niche, I call it a Brilliant Idea. What's yours?

**Follow-ups (use 1-2 based on answer):**
- If vague: "Push past the first obvious answer. If you had to get really specific — what kind of person, what kind of result?"
- If two ideas: "If you had to pick the ONE — which one wakes you up at 3 a.m.?"
- If story-rich: "Tell me the moment you knew this was it."

**LOCK IT IN:**
> Say it back to me in one sentence — "I love X, I'm good at Y, so my Brilliant Idea is Z."

**CAPTURE:** the locked one-sentence idea. This goes into intake notes.

---

## MIN 12–22 · MODULE 2 — WHO ARE YOU?

**TAB SAYS:**

> Okay. Now let's get into your brand. Five quick questions.

**Q1 — Who are you?**
> If you walked into a room — how would someone describe you? Your story, your energy, your vibe. Don't perform — just tell me.

**Q2 — What do you actually do?**
> Plain language. What do you literally do? Not your title — your actions.

**Q3 — Who do you do it for?**
> And get specific. Not "entrepreneurs," not "small businesses." What KIND of person? What stage?

**Q4 — Why do you do it?**
> What would disappoint you if this business didn't exist?

**Q5 — Why do you stand out?**
> Your story plus your method plus your results. What's your edge?

**Q6 — How do you do it? (Your secret sauce)**
> Your specific way. The thing you do that nobody else does the same.

**LOCK IT IN:**
> One line: "I help [WHO] achieve [WHAT] through [HOW]."

**CAPTURE:** Q1-Q6 verbatim. The one-liner. Energy/tone of voice cues (note in margin: "talked fast about Q3 = passionate," "paused on Q4 = personal," etc.).

---

## MIN 22–30 · MODULE 3 — WHO ARE THEY?

**TAB SAYS:**

> Let's talk about who you serve. Three layers.

**Layer 1 — Demographics:**
> Age, life stage, income level, business stage, what they do, what they've been doing.

**Layer 2 — Psychographics:**
> What do they value? What keeps them up at night? What have they already tried? What do they say when they're stuck?

**Layer 3 — Where to find them:**
> Where do they hang out? Platforms, podcasts, communities, events. Who do they follow?

**Follow-up to push for cultural specificity (this is the move):**
> Don't give me a demographic — give me a cultural snapshot. What show did they watch growing up? What music plays when they're working? Where did they go to school? Where do they get takeout from?

**LOCK IT IN:**
> "My ideal client is [SPECIFIC PERSON]. Their #1 pain is [PAIN]. I reach them at [PLATFORM/PLACE]."

**CAPTURE:** all three layers. The cultural touchstones. Any specific names/brands they mention (these become Dream 100 candidates).

---

## MIN 30–35 · BREAK / PIVOT MOMENT

**TAB SAYS:**

> Okay — we're halfway. Take a sip of water. Anything come up so far you want to add or correct?

**CAPTURE:** corrections. Often the best brand stuff lands here.

---

## MIN 35–43 · MODULE 4 — WHAT DO THEY NEED? (Offering)

**TAB SAYS:**

> Now your offer. Six questions. By the end we'll have one clear sellable package.

1. Who is it for?
2. What problem does it solve?
3. What IS it? Format, length, delivery.
4. What does it take YOU to deliver? Your inputs.
5. What are the deliverables? List everything.
6. What is the OUTCOME? The transformation.

**The pricing question:**
> Pressure test — would YOU pay for this outcome? At what price?

**LOCK IT IN:**
> "I offer [NAME] — a [FORMAT] for [WHO], that helps them [PROBLEM], by delivering [DELIVERABLES], so they can [OUTCOME]. Investment: $[PRICE]."

**CAPTURE:** all 6 answers. The locked offer statement. The price. Tab's gut check: does this feel sellable?

---

## MIN 43–50 · MODULE 5 — THE NARRATIVE (Your Story Is Your Strategy)

**TAB SAYS:**

> Let's talk about your story. Not the polished version — the real one.

**Part 1 — Origin + Why + Struggle:**
> How did you start? Why does this matter to you personally? What did you go through to figure it out?

**Part 2 — Who you serve + why you feel called to them:**
> What connects YOUR story to THEIR struggle? Why them?

**Part 3 — Why your offer is the solution:**
> Why is what you built the answer to their specific problem? What makes your method THE one?

**Follow-up to find the hook line:**
> If you had to pull ONE sentence from everything you just said — the one that makes someone say "that's me" — what is it?

**LOCK IT IN:**
> The hook line, in one sentence.

**CAPTURE:** all 3 parts (long-form quotes). The hook line. Any specific phrases the customer uses that should be preserved verbatim in their brand voice.

---

## MIN 50–55 · MODULE 6 — CONTENT + GOALS

**TAB SAYS:**

> Last module. Quick.

**Q1 — Goal:**
> What's the ONE thing you most want this brand to accomplish in the next six months? Be specific. A number, a date, a milestone.

**Q2 — Inspirations (the competitor question Tab flagged):**
> Who do you wish to emulate? What brands do you admire? Not because you want to copy them — because they're getting it right in a way you respect.

**Q3 — Holidays / Content rhythm:**
> Any upcoming launches, holidays, or events I should know about for your content plan?

**Q4 — Existing assets:**
> Anything you've already built that should carry forward? Existing logo, fonts you love, color preferences, photos? Send me anything in the email thread after this call.

**CAPTURE:** the 6-month goal. The 2-3 admired brands (these become competitor analysis WS6 inputs). Content calendar anchors. Asset upload list.

---

## MIN 55–60 · CLOSE + NEXT STEPS

**TAB SAYS:**

> Okay. That was a lot. Take a breath.
>
> Here's what happens next:
>
> 1. Tonight or tomorrow, I take everything you just shared into my system. The AI does the first pass — your messaging, logo concepts, brand kit, GTM checklist, [+ content + full suite stuff if applicable].
> 2. I personally review every line and every choice before you see anything.
> 3. You'll get your full Brand Blueprint delivered by email by [DAY OF WEEK + DATE — 5 business days out].
> 4. After delivery, you get one refinement pass — if something needs to land different, you tell me, I fix it.
>
> Any questions?
>
> Last thing — if you have any assets to send (existing logo, photos you want included, links to brands you mentioned) — reply to my email and drop them in.
>
> Thank you for trusting me with this. Talk soon.

**POST-CALL (within 60 min):**
- [ ] Stop recording. Save with customer name + date.
- [ ] Type intake into `/new/deep` form
- [ ] Note the call vibe (1-3 lines) — this informs voice/tone choices
- [ ] Send delivery-timeline email (template in `email-templates.md`)
- [ ] Block calendar time for review (per tier — 2 hrs Basic, 3.5 hrs Content, 5 hrs Full)

---

## ENERGY / PACING NOTES

- The script is a SAFETY NET. If the customer is rolling, follow the energy. Don't interrupt momentum to hit Q3 if Q2 turned into 8 min of gold.
- The MIDDLE is where the real brand lives. Modules 1, 5, and 6 produce the soul. Module 3 produces the specificity. Module 2 + 4 produce the structure. If you have to cut for time, trim Module 2 questions — those can be inferred. Never cut Modules 1, 3, 5.
- LISTEN for verbatim phrases the customer uses. Those phrases become the brand voice. Write them down literally — don't paraphrase.
- WATCH for the moment they tear up or get fired up. That's the brand story core. Flag it in your notes.

---

## RED FLAGS DURING THE CALL

If you see any of these, address before continuing:

| Red flag | What to do |
|---|---|
| Customer keeps saying "I don't know" to everything | Pause. Ask: "What's going on? Are you in your head or not in the right space?" Reschedule if needed. |
| Customer is in the middle of a major life event (divorce, grief, crisis) | Acknowledge. Ask if they want to reschedule. The brand work requires emotional bandwidth. |
| Customer is asking for things outside their tier | Note it. Don't promise. Offer to upgrade after delivery. |
| Customer's "brilliant idea" is borderline illegal or harmful | Stop. Refund. Don't proceed. |
| Customer is openly hostile or dismissive | Trust your gut. You're allowed to refund and walk away. |
